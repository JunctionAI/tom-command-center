## 1. EUGENE SCHWARTZ — Applied to Pet Parents (Awareness Levels)

**Same master as Meridian, but applied through the pet lens. The awareness levels 
shift when you're selling to someone on behalf of their pet.**

**Pet Parent Awareness Levels:**

1. **Most Aware:** "I know Pure Pets, I've bought before, just give me a deal."
   → Retention emails, loyalty rewards, subscription offers.

2. **Product Aware:** "I've heard of Pure Pets but haven't tried it."
   → Testimonials from other pet parents, before/after videos, guarantee.

3. **Solution Aware:** "I know joint supplements exist for dogs, but which one?"
   → Mechanism differentiation (NZ Green Lipped Mussel vs glucosamine), vet endorsement, 
   ingredient comparison.

4. **Problem Aware:** "My dog is limping / slowing down / can't jump on the couch."
   → Lead with the EMOTION. The guilt of watching your pet suffer. Then introduce 
   the solution. This is where UGC video content dominates.

5. **Unaware:** "I don't realise my dog's slowing down is treatable."
   → Educational content. "5 signs your dog's joints need help." Native-style content 
   that doesn't feel like an ad.

**The critical insight for Pure Pets:** Most pet parents are Level 4. They SEE the 
problem every day. The emotional trigger is already loaded. The ad just needs to 
connect: "You see your dog struggling. Here's what thousands of pet parents did 
about it."

## 2. EMILY WEISS / GLOSSIER — The DTC Community Playbook

**Built Glossier from a blog into a $1.8B brand by turning customers into a community 
that markets the product for you.**

**Principles that apply to Pure Pets:**

- **Customers ARE the marketing:** The most powerful marketing is a real person sharing 
  their real experience. UGC from actual pet parents with their actual dogs beats any 
  professional ad.
- **Build in public:** Share the journey. Product development, ingredient sourcing, 
  customer stories. Let people feel like insiders.
- **The "top of funnel is the product" model:** Make the content so good that people 
  share it regardless of whether they buy. Pet transformation videos get shared by 
  EVERY dog lover, not just supplement buyers.
- **Community before conversion:** Build a following of pet parents who trust you, 
  then selling becomes natural. Don't lead with sales.

**For Pure Pets UGC strategy:**
- Real dogs, real owners, real stories — not stock footage
- The emotional arc: suffering → discovery → transformation
- User-submitted content (incentivise customers to share videos)
- Community building: "The Pure Pets Pack" or similar

## 3. RYAN HOLIDAY — Perennial Seller (Making Things That Last)

**Strategist who's sold millions of books and advised some of the most successful 
creative launches. His framework for products that sell for years, not weeks.**

**The Perennial Seller framework applied to Pure Pets:**

- **The product must be genuinely great.** No amount of marketing saves a bad product. 
  The GLM supplement must actually work. Every marketing dollar spent on a mediocre 
  product is wasted. Every dollar spent on a great product compounds.
- **Word of mouth is the only channel that scales for free.** Design for it. Make the 
  unboxing memorable. Make the results visible. Make sharing easy.
- **Positioning is a long game.** Don't chase trends. Position Pure Pets as THE NZ 
  premium joint supplement for pets. Own that position for decades.
- **One customer at a time:** "The best marketing strategy is to care about your 
  customers more than anyone else." Respond to every review. Follow up after purchase. 
  Send handwritten notes to your first 100 customers.

## 4. DANIEL KAHNEMAN — Behavioural Economics Applied to Pet Purchasing

**Nobel laureate who revealed the cognitive biases that drive human decision-making. 
Critical for understanding WHY pet parents buy.**

**Key biases in pet supplement purchasing:**

- **Loss aversion (2x stronger than gain):** "Don't let your dog lose their mobility" 
  is 2x more motivating than "Help your dog gain mobility." Frame as PREVENTING loss.

- **Social proof:** "10,000+ dogs transformed" works because pet parents feel safer 
  following what other parents did. Especially powerful with visual proof (videos, 
  photos).

- **Identifiable victim effect:** ONE specific dog's story (with name, photo, journey) 
  is more emotionally powerful than "thousands of dogs helped." Teddy's story > statistics.

- **The IKEA effect:** If customers feel involved in the process (choosing the right 
  supplement, tracking progress, sharing results), they value the product more. 
  → Create a "joint health tracker" or "progress diary."

- **Anchoring:** Show the premium option first. When they see the $89 option before 
  the $49 option, the $49 feels like a deal. Price anchoring in product pages matters.

- **Guilt as motivator:** Pet parents feel responsible for their pet's quality of life. 
  This is the most powerful emotional lever in the entire category. Use it with 
  compassion, never manipulation. "They can't choose for themselves. You can."

## 5. SABRI SUBY — Sell Like Crazy (DTC Performance Marketing)

**Built Australia's fastest-growing digital agency. His framework is specifically 
designed for DTC brands spending on Meta/Google.**

**The "Godfather Offer" framework:**
Create an offer so compelling, your ideal customer would feel stupid saying no.

For Pure Pets:
- Not just "buy joint supplements" but "Get your dog's mobility back — or pay nothing"
- Risk reversal: 90-day money-back guarantee (works because the product actually works)
- Stack the value: Supplement + dosage guide + progress tracker + vet consultation 
  checklist
- Create urgency: Limited first-batch pricing, seasonal promotions

**The HBSP (Hook-Believability-Social Proof-Push) formula for ads:**
1. **Hook:** Stop the scroll with the emotional trigger (limping dog → running dog)
2. **Believability:** Why this works (NZ Green Lipped Mussel, specific mechanism)
3. **Social Proof:** Other pet parents' results (testimonials, numbers)
4. **Push:** Clear CTA with risk reversal ("Try it risk-free for 90 days")

**The "Dream 100" method:**
Identify the 100 people/pages/groups where your ideal customers already gather.
For Pure Pets: Dog breed Facebook groups, pet health forums, vet recommendation 
pages, dog training communities, pet influencers.

## 6. RUSSELL BRUNSON — Sales Funnels & The Value Ladder

**Built ClickFunnels to $100M+ by understanding the psychology of online purchasing. 
His value ladder concept is critical for supplement subscription models.**

**The Value Ladder for Pure Pets:**

```
Level 1 (FREE): Educational content — "5 Signs of Joint Problems" PDF
                → Captures email. Builds trust.

Level 2 ($19-29): Starter pack / trial size
                → Low barrier to entry. Customer experiences the product.

Level 3 ($49-79): Full-size product (one-time purchase)
                → Standard sale. The main conversion target.

Level 4 ($39-69/month): Subscription (auto-ship)
                → Recurring revenue. The real business model.
                → Must be easy to pause/cancel (trust)

Level 5 ($99+): Premium bundle (multi-pet, with extras)
                → For the most committed pet parents.
                → "Complete Joint Health System"
```

**Key insight:** Don't try to sell Level 4 to someone at Level 0. The ladder exists 
because trust must be built incrementally. Lead magnets → trial → purchase → subscribe.

**For email marketing specifically:**
- Welcome sequence moves people UP the ladder
- Abandoned cart catches people falling OFF the ladder
- Post-purchase nurture locks in the current rung before pushing higher
- Winback reaches down to pull people back ON the ladder

## SYNTHESIS: THE SCOUT DECISION STACK

### Before creating ANY content:
1. **Schwartz** — What awareness level is this audience?
2. **Kahneman** — Which cognitive bias are we activating?
3. **Suby** — Does this follow HBSP (Hook-Believability-Social Proof-Push)?
4. **Weiss** — Does this feel authentic enough that a real pet parent would share it?

### Before launching ANY campaign:
1. **Brunson** — Where does this sit on the value ladder?
2. **Holiday** — Does this build long-term brand or just extract short-term revenue?
3. **DBH proven formulas** — Are we using Trust+Social Proof, Exclusivity+Scarcity, 
   or Gift Psychology adapted for pets?

### For UGC video content specifically:
1. Lead with the VISIBLE PROBLEM (Level 4 awareness — dog struggling)
2. Show the TRANSFORMATION (before/after — most powerful format)
3. Feature REAL OWNERS with their real dogs (Weiss community model)
4. Include ONE specific proof point (Kahneman identifiable victim)
5. End with risk-reversal offer (Suby godfather offer)
