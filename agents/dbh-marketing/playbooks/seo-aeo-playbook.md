## PROVEN WINNERS — SEARCH OPTIMIZATION

### 1. The Comparison Formula (Highest Commercial Intent ROAS)
**Pattern:** "[Product] vs [Alternative]" → Educational comparison → Product positioning
**Evidence:** "Green lipped mussel vs glucosamine" (150-300 monthly searches, 3.4 priority score)
**Why it works:**
- Highest purchase intent keyword type (searcher is deciding between options)
- Allows DBH to position GLM as superior alternative
- Naturally leads to product recommendation

**Article Structure:**
1. **Intro** (150 words) — "You're deciding between two of the most popular joint supplements. Here's the real difference..."
2. **Comparison Table** (200 words) — Side-by-side: mechanism, research, timeline, cost, side effects
3. **Deep Dive Section 1** (300 words) — "How Green Lipped Mussel Works" (include TRUST + SOCIAL PROOF formula: real customer result)
4. **Deep Dive Section 2** (300 words) — "How Glucosamine Works" (fair comparison; don't strawman)
5. **Why We Choose GLM** (200 words) — Clinical angle + customer testimonial
6. **FAQ Section** (200 words) — Schema-friendly questions
7. **CTA** (50 words) — Soft: "See customer results" → product page

**Creative Formula Applied:** TRUST + SOCIAL PROOF
- Must include 1-2 customer testimonials showing GLM results
- Specific: "Joint pain reduced 43% in 8 weeks" (not generic "customers love it")
- Link to product with real customer before/after imagery

**KPI Targets:**
- First ranking: Within 45 days (top 20)
- First page ranking: Within 90 days
- Organic traffic: 150-300 monthly (sustained after ranking)
- Click-through rate: 3-5% (high commercial intent)
- Product page conversion: 1.5-2% (high-intent traffic)

**Confidence:** 95% — Comparison keywords proven across supplement category

### 2. The Benefit-Specific Formula (Highest Engagement + Demographic Targeting)
**Pattern:** "[Product] for [Specific Benefit/Condition]" → Educational guide → Product fit
**Evidence:** "Green lipped mussel for joint pain relief" (100-200 monthly, 2.9 priority score), "Marine collagen for aging skin" (150-300 monthly, 2.7 priority score)
**Why it works:**
- Searcher already knows the benefit they want (not comparing options)
- Highly specific content ranks for long-tail variations too
- Natural place to include demographic angles (age, lifestyle)

**Article Structure:**
1. **Problem Intro** (200 words) — "If you have [condition], you've probably tried [obvious solutions]..."
2. **Why This Benefit Matters** (200 words) — Emotional angle + science summary
3. **How [Product] Specifically Addresses This** (400 words) — Mechanism + timeline
4. **Customer Proof Section** (200 words) — 2 detailed testimonials with timeline + specific result
5. **How to Use It** (200 words) — Dosage, timeline to results, complementary products
6. **FAQ + Schema** (200 words) — Common questions from searchers
7. **CTA** (50 words) — "Shop [Product]" + results guarantee link

**Creative Formula Applied:** BENEFIT-SPECIFIC MESSAGING + SOCIAL PROOF
- Subject/headline must name the SPECIFIC benefit ("Joint Pain Relief" not "Joint Health")
- Lead with the emotional benefit, then the mechanism
- Real testimonials (not stock imagery): "How I fixed my joint pain" format

**Segment Integration:**
For "over 50" demographic articles, reference customer-segments-playbook:
- Audience: Over-50 demographic (high purchase intent for health supplements)
- Email follow-up: If article visitor emails list, trigger "Joint Health for Active 50+" flow
- Product recommendation: Include complementary products (collagen + GLM combo)

**KPI Targets:**
- First ranking: Within 60 days (top 30)
- Sustained ranking: Top 5 within 4 months (evergreen content)
- Organic traffic: 100-300 monthly (long-tail traffic + main keyword)
- Segment conversion: 1-2% (demographic-specific, higher LTV)
- Product bundle conversion: 1.5-2.5% (multi-product articles outconvert)

**Confidence:** 90% — Proven across Meta/Email; translates to SEO

### 3. The Authority Guide Formula (Highest Citation Potential + AEO Opportunity)
**Pattern:** "[Product] Benefits: Complete Guide" → Comprehensive → Schema + citations
**Evidence:** "Green lipped mussel benefits" (300-800 monthly, 2.6 priority score, AI Overview target)
**Why it works:**
- Authority queries attract AI Overview consideration
- Comprehensive structure gets cited in AI Overviews
- Drives high-quality traffic (even if not top ranking, featured in AI)

**Article Structure:**
1. **Introduction** (250 words) — What GLM is + why people care
2. **The Clinical Research** (400 words) — Studies cited, results, mechanism
3. **Specific Benefits Breakdown** (600 words) — Joint health, anti-inflammatory, skin, gut (multiple benefits)
4. **Timeline: What to Expect** (200 words) — Week 1, month 1, month 3 (very specific)
5. **Real Customer Results** (300 words) — 3 testimonials with timeline
6. **Safety + Side Effects** (200 words) — Honest; builds trust
7. **FAQ with Schema** (300 words) — 8-10 questions answering common queries
8. **How It Fits Your Routine** (200 words) — Combinations with other products
9. **CTA** (50 words) — Link to product + research summary

**Creative Formula Applied:** TRUST + SOCIAL PROOF + EDUCATIONAL AUTHORITY
- Must include medical/scientific credibility signals
- Author credentials (if available)
- Clinical study citations (with links)
- FAQ schema (JSON-LD structured data)
- Customer testimonials with specific before/after, timeline

**AEO Optimization Specific:**
- Use FAQ schema on every section (AI Overviews prefer FAQ structure)
- Include direct answer paragraphs (AI extracts these)
- Cite research studies (medical sources credible to AI)
- Include definition box at top (AI often uses this)
- Structure benefits as numbered list (AI easy parsing)

**Segment Integration:**
This content targets broad audiences. Reference customer-segments-playbook:
- Audience: New subscribers (education-first appeal)
- Engagement: Share on social for awareness stage
- Email: Use for nurture sequences, not immediate sales
- Product link: Soft (this is authority-building, not direct sell)

**KPI Targets:**
- First ranking: Within 90 days (top 30, authority plays slower)
- AI Overview appearance: Within 6 months (if structure correct)
- Sustained ranking: Top 5-10 within 6 months
- Organic traffic: 200-500 monthly (broad keyword, authority content)
- AI Overview citation: 10-20% traffic lift when appearing
- Click-through: 2-3% (high-value traffic from awareness stage)

**Confidence:** 85% — Authority content strategy proven; AEO relatively new

## DISPROVEN APPROACHES (Do NOT Use)

### ❌ Generic Wellness Content
**Why It Failed:** "Your Health Journey" or "Wellness Tips" articles rank poorly and convert at 0.2-0.3%.
**Evidence:** DBH historical blog content lacking benefit specificity underperformed
**Action:** Every article must have SPECIFIC benefit in title/headline

### ❌ Product-First Content (No Customer Proof)
**Why It Failed:** "About Green Lipped Mussel Supplement" pages without testimonials get high bounce rate (70%+)
**Evidence:** Product pages rank but don't convert without social proof
**Action:** Every article must include customer testimonials, timeline, specific result

### ❌ Keyword Stuffing / Thin Content
**Why It Failed:** "Green lipped mussel supplement supplement supplement" = penalty risk + poor UX
**Evidence:** 2026 Google algorithm prioritizes helpful, original content
**Action:** Target 2000+ word minimum; focus on searcher intent, not keyword count

### ❌ Unsubstantiated Health Claims
**Why It Failed:** NZ TAPS/ASA violations = content takedown, credibility loss
**Evidence:** Compliance issues documented in previous playbooks
**Action:** Use "supports," "helps maintain," "may help"; cite studies; never "cures" or "treats"

### ❌ Competitor Strawman Comparisons
**Why It Failed:** Unfair comparisons hurt credibility when trying to build authority
**Evidence:** Readers trust balanced content more than biased advocacy
**Action:** Comparison articles must be fair; acknowledge competitor strengths while highlighting DBH advantages

### ❌ Ignoring Mobile UX
**Why It Failed:** 60%+ of searchers on mobile; slow load times = bounce
**Evidence:** Page speed is ranking factor
**Action:** All articles must be mobile-optimized; images compressed; structure scannable on mobile
