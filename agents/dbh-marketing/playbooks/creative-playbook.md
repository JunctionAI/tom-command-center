## PROVEN WINNERS — CROSS-CHANNEL

### 1. The Trust Formula (Highest Sustainable ROAS)
**Pattern:** Authentic testimonial + specific result + trust signals
**Meta Evidence:** GLM Social Proof + Trust — 7.78x ROAS, $2,405 revenue, 25 conversions from $309 spend
**Email Evidence:** "Discover What Sets Us Apart" — 48.53% open, 2.95% click, $1,067 from 963 recipients
**Why it works:** Removes the #1 purchase objection ("does it actually work?"). Customer voice > brand voice. Attracts quality customers with higher LTV, not price-shoppers.
**Creative spec:**
- META: Real customer photo (not model/stock), headline with specific result ("joint pain reduced in 3 weeks"), primary text tells their story, CTA = "See Results"
- EMAIL: Subject line references customer experience, body = story → proof → how to replicate → soft CTA
- NEVER: Use generic stock imagery, make unsubstantiated claims, fake testimonials
**Confidence:** 95% — validated across both channels with consistent results

### 2. The Scarcity Formula (Highest Peak ROAS)
**Pattern:** Limited time + genuine deadline + clear value proposition
**Meta Evidence:** Black Friday 15% Off — 8.45x ROAS on $17.76 spend
**Email Evidence:** "Early Access Reminder" — $9,259 revenue (highest single email); "BFCM Last Chance" — $4,471, 42.3% open
**Why it works:** Behavioral economics — loss aversion is 2x stronger than gain motivation. Time pressure forces action from people already interested but procrastinating.
**Creative spec:**
- META: High-contrast urgency visual, countdown or "ends in X hours", discount badge, strong CTA
- EMAIL: Subject with deadline ("Final Hours"), countdown element in body, single bold CTA, loss-framing ("Don't miss out")
- RULE: Only use for genuine scarcity. Max 4-6 scarcity campaigns per year. Overuse kills credibility.
**Confidence:** 90% — strongest during seasonal peaks, less data for manufactured scarcity

### 3. The Gift Formula (Highest Email Engagement)
**Pattern:** Bonus/gift framing instead of discount framing
**Email Evidence:** "Spend $150, get free moisturiser" — 77.1% open rate, 4.15% click rate
**Why it works:** Same economic value as a discount but different psychological frame. "You're getting something extra" (reward) vs "You're paying less" (transaction). Gift framing creates positive emotion and eliminates discount fatigue.
**Creative spec:**
- EMAIL: Subject = "Your free [X] is waiting" or "Spend $[Y], get [Z] free", hero image of the gift product, framing emphasizes what they GAIN not what they SAVE
- META: Gift product prominently shown alongside main product, "FREE [X] with purchase" overlay
- RULE: Gift item should feel premium and desirable, not clearance stock. Perceived value matters more than actual cost.
**Confidence:** 90% — strong single data point, needs more testing across product categories

## PROVEN WINNERS — META-SPECIFIC

### 4. Testimonial Image + Vibrant Color + Trust Badge
**Evidence:** Combined analysis — GLM Social Proof + Trust campaign with highest engagement
**Formula:** Real customer photo + vibrant/warm color palette + "Made in NZ" or pharmaceutical-grade trust badge
**Creative spec:** Customer image (real, not polished) + product in context + trust badge bottom-right + warm color overlay
**Why it works:** Authenticity cuts through ad fatigue. Trust badge addresses supplement credibility concern. Color draws attention in feed.
**Confidence:** 90%

### 5. Tiered Bundle Presentation
**Evidence:** GLM Tiered Bundle — 4.06x ROAS, $31,284 revenue
**Formula:** Show multiple bundle options with clear value progression
**Creative spec:** Side-by-side bundle comparison (Good/Better/Best), savings highlighted per tier, most popular tier visually emphasized
**Why it works:** Gives customer agency. Anchoring effect — middle tier feels like best value.
**Confidence:** 85%

## PROVEN WINNERS — EMAIL-SPECIFIC

### 6. Benefit-Specific > Generic Wellness
**Evidence:** "Support Your Joints Naturally" — 52.85% open vs generic at 15-30%
**Rule:** Every subject line must name a SPECIFIC benefit, body part, or outcome
**Good:** "Support Your Joints Naturally 🌿" / "Better Sleep Starts Tonight" / "Your Skin After 30 Days of Marine Collagen"
**Bad:** "Your Health Journey" / "Wellness Update" / "New from Deep Blue Health"
**Confidence:** 95%

### 7. Segment-Matched Frequency and Tone
**Evidence:** 90-Day Engaged = 45-52% open (benefit messaging). Newsletter = 15-30% open (needs harder hooks).
**Rule:** Match creative approach to segment warmth
- WARM (90-day engaged, repeat buyers): Benefit/value/recommendation. No discounts needed.
- COOL (newsletter, new subscribers): Curiosity hooks, social proof, offers needed.
- COLD (lapsed, winback): Scarcity, "we miss you", strong incentive.
**Confidence:** 95%

## EMERGING PATTERNS
*Promising but needs more testing*

### 1. Education-Then-Sell Sequence
- **Signal:** Educational content (84.4% open rate) followed by product recommendation could create high-trust conversion path
- **Hypothesis:** 3-email sequence: Educate → Social proof → Offer would outperform single promotional email
- **Test plan:** Build 3-part GLM education series, measure sequence completion rate and final email conversion
- **Confidence:** 65%

### 2. Post-Purchase Recommendation Without Discount
- **Signal:** 47.52% open, 3.72% click, $1,807 revenue — customers don't need discounts post-purchase
- **Hypothesis:** Recommendation flow based on purchase history drives repeat purchase at full margin
- **Test plan:** Build "Complete Your Routine" post-purchase flow, A/B against discount-based cross-sell
- **Confidence:** 75%

### 3. Manufactured Scarcity Outside Seasonal Peaks
- **Signal:** Seasonal scarcity = 8.45x ROAS. Can we replicate outside BFCM?
- **Hypothesis:** Flash sales (48-hour window), limited-edition bundles, or "last X units" could create non-seasonal urgency
- **Test plan:** Run one flash sale in a non-seasonal month, compare ROAS to baseline
- **Confidence:** 60%

### 4. AI-Generated UGC Video Testimonials
- **Signal:** Real testimonials work. AI video tools (Veo 3.1, Sora) are approaching realistic quality.
- **Hypothesis:** AI UGC that looks authentic could scale the testimonial formula without content collection bottleneck
- **Test plan:** Create 3 AI testimonial ads, A/B against real testimonial ads on Meta
- **Confidence:** 55%

## DISPROVEN HYPOTHESES
*Do not repeat these approaches*

### 1. Discount-Only Meta Ads
- **Evidence:** All discount-only Meta campaigns delivered under 2.5x ROAS
- **Learning:** Discounts attract price-shoppers with low LTV. Always pair discount with trust/social proof angle. Discount should be the cherry, not the cake.

### 2. Interest-Based Meta Targeting (Joint Health, Supplements)
- **Evidence:** Broad targeting with strong creative outperformed interest targeting
- **Learning:** Let the creative do the targeting. Meta's algorithm finds buyers better than manual interest stacks when the creative clearly signals who it's for.

### 3. Generic Newsletter Blasts Without Segmentation
- **Evidence:** 15-25% open rates vs 45-52% for segmented sends
- **Learning:** "Send to everyone" is lazy, not strategic. Always segment by engagement tier minimum.

### 4. Education-Only Without Social Proof or CTA
- **Evidence:** Educational Meta ads: 2.38x ROAS vs 7.78x when combined with testimonials
- **Learning:** Education builds awareness but doesn't convert alone. Always pair education with customer proof or clear CTA for conversion.

## THE DBH CREATIVE DECISION TREE

```
Starting a new campaign?
│
├─ What's the goal?
│  ├─ REVENUE NOW → Use Formula 1 (Trust) or Formula 2 (Scarcity)
│  ├─ ENGAGEMENT/NURTURE → Use Formula 3 (Gift) or Educational content
│  └─ PRODUCT LAUNCH → Use New Product + Discount to broad, Benefit to engaged
│
├─ What channel?
│  ├─ META → Lead with testimonial image + trust signals
│  ├─ EMAIL (engaged) → Lead with benefit/value, no discount
│  └─ EMAIL (broad) → Lead with hook/curiosity + offer
│
├─ What segment?
│  ├─ WARM → Benefit narrative, recommendations, exclusivity
│  ├─ COOL → Social proof, curiosity hooks, moderate offers
│  └─ COLD → Strong scarcity, generous offers, win-back framing
│
└─ Check: Does the creative include at least one of:
   ├─ ✅ Specific benefit claim (not generic wellness)
   ├─ ✅ Social proof / testimonial
   ├─ ✅ Clear single CTA
   └─ If none: REVISE before sending
```

## COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS
- NZ TAPS/ASA standards: "supports" and "helps maintain" — never "cures" or "treats"
- Testimonials must be genuine, verifiable customer experiences
- "Made in New Zealand" and "pharmaceutical-grade" claims must be substantiated
- Meta ad copy must comply with Facebook health advertising policies (no before/after body images, no personal health attribute targeting)
- Google Ads supplement restrictions apply — no guarantees of efficacy
