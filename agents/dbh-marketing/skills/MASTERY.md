## I. THE MASTERS YOU THINK LIKE

### Robert Cialdini (Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion)
**Core insight:** Human compliance follows six predictable principles. Master them and you master conversion.
1. **Reciprocity** — Give value first. Free guides, samples, knowledge. The customer feels obligated to reciprocate.
2. **Commitment & Consistency** — Get a small yes (email signup, quiz), then escalate. People want to be consistent with past actions.
3. **Social Proof** — "12,847 customers trust their joints to GLM" matters more than ANY feature list. Testimonials > claims.
4. **Authority** — Vet endorsements, clinical studies, "recommended by practitioners" — trust signals from credible sources.
5. **Liking** — People buy from brands they like. Warmth, relatability, shared values. UGC > polished ads because it feels HUMAN.
6. **Scarcity** — "Only 47 left" works because loss aversion is twice as powerful as gain anticipation. But ONLY if genuine.

**Application to DBH:** Your three proven formulas MAP directly to Cialdini:
- Trust+Social Proof (7.78x ROAS) = Social Proof + Authority
- Exclusivity+Scarcity (8.45x ROAS) = Scarcity + Commitment
- Gift Psychology (77.1% open rate) = Reciprocity

### Al Ries & Jack Trout (Positioning)
**Core insight:** Marketing is not a battle of products. It's a battle of perceptions. Own a WORD in the customer's mind.
- **The Law of Focus:** Own one word. DBH = "New Zealand marine supplements." Pure Pets = "visible pet mobility."
- **The Law of Category:** If you can't be first in a category, create a new category you CAN be first in.
- **The Law of the Mind:** It's better to be first in the mind than first in the market.
- **Application:** Don't compete with Swisse on "vitamins." Compete on "NZ marine-sourced joint health."

### Drew Sanocki / Andrew Foxwell (DTC Playbook)
**Core insight:** DTC profitability comes from three levers: increase frequency (F), increase average order value (AOV), and increase customer count (C). But F × AOV is almost always more profitable than increasing C.
- **The RFM Framework:** Recency, Frequency, Monetary value. Segment customers by these three dimensions.
- **Whale hunting:** Your top 10% of customers generate 40-60% of revenue. Know them. Serve them differently.
- **The 60-day rule:** If a customer doesn't reorder within 60 days, they're slipping. Win-back flows must trigger.
- **CAC payback period:** If you can't recover customer acquisition cost within 60 days, your funnel is broken.

### Eugene Schwartz (Breakthrough Advertising)
**Core insight:** You cannot create desire. You can only channel existing desire toward your product. The question is: what is the customer's STATE OF AWARENESS?
- **5 Stages of Awareness:**
  1. **Unaware** — Don't know they have a problem → Lead with the problem, not the product
  2. **Problem-Aware** — Know the problem, don't know solutions exist → Agitate the pain
  3. **Solution-Aware** — Know solutions exist, don't know your product → Differentiate
  4. **Product-Aware** — Know your product, haven't bought → Overcome objections, prove it works
  5. **Most Aware** — Past customers → Just make the offer. They trust you already.
- **Application:** Different ad creative for EACH stage. Top-of-funnel ≠ retargeting copy.

### Sabri Suby (Sell Like Crazy)
**Core insight:** The "Godfather Offer" — make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no. Lead with value, not with the product.
- **The HBSO Method:** High-value Bribe → Sell Once (don't sell in every touchpoint)
- **Dream 100:** Identify your 100 best potential customers/partners and focus ALL efforts on them
- **Application:** Lead magnet (joint health guide) → Email nurture → Offer with risk reversal (money-back guarantee)

### Chase Dimond (DTC Email Marketing)
**Core insight:** Email is the highest-ROI channel in DTC because you OWN the relationship. Every $1 in email = $36-$45 return.
- **The 80/20 of email:** 20% of flows generate 80% of revenue. Welcome series, abandoned cart, post-purchase, and win-back are non-negotiable.
- **Subject line hierarchy:** Curiosity > Benefit > Urgency > Personalisation
- **Send frequency:** More is more (within reason). 4-5 campaigns/week for engaged segments.
- **Segmentation is everything:** Engaged (opened in 30 days), At-risk (30-60 days), Dormant (60+ days). Different content for each.

## II. ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORKS

### The DTC Growth Equation
```
Revenue = Traffic × Conversion Rate × AOV × Purchase Frequency
```
Every strategy should attack one of these four levers. Know which one is the bottleneck NOW.

### The North Star Metrics (by stage)
- **Pre-PMF:** Repeat purchase rate (>30% = you have something)
- **Growth stage:** CAC payback period (<60 days = scalable)
- **Scale stage:** Contribution margin after ad spend (>30% = profitable)
- **Mature stage:** LTV:CAC ratio (>3:1 = healthy)

### The Creative Testing Framework
1. **Concept tests** (5-10 variations) — Test the MESSAGE, not the creative quality
2. **Format tests** — UGC vs polished, video vs static, long vs short
3. **Hook tests** — First 3 seconds determine everything in video ads
4. **Audience tests** — Same winning creative to different audiences
5. **Scale winners** — Only increase budget on creatives with 3+ days of consistent performance

### The Full-Funnel Attribution Model
- **TOFU (Top of Funnel):** Awareness. Measured by reach, CPM, thumbstop rate. Don't judge ROAS here.
- **MOFU (Middle):** Consideration. Measured by engagement, email signups, content consumption.
- **BOFU (Bottom):** Conversion. Measured by ROAS, CPA, conversion rate.
- **Post-Purchase:** Retention. Measured by repeat rate, LTV, NPS.
- **Key insight:** Judging TOFU by BOFU metrics kills your pipeline. Each stage has different success metrics.

## III. SUPPLEMENT-SPECIFIC MASTERY

### The Health Claims Tightrope
- NEVER claim to cure, treat, diagnose, or prevent disease
- ALWAYS use: "supports," "helps maintain," "contributes to"
- Structure-function claims are safe: "supports joint health" ✅
- Disease claims are illegal: "treats arthritis" ❌
- Social proof sidesteps claim restrictions: "My dog can run again" (customer testimonial) carries the message without making a brand claim

### The Trust Pyramid for Supplements
```
Level 1: INGREDIENT PROOF — Clinical studies, published research
Level 2: MANUFACTURING PROOF — GMP certified, tested, NZ-made
Level 3: SOCIAL PROOF — Reviews, testimonials, before/after
Level 4: AUTHORITY PROOF — Vet/practitioner endorsements
Level 5: BRAND PROOF — Years in business, heritage, story
```
Stack ALL levels. Each layer compounds trust.

### The Subscription Economics
- Subscription customers have 3-5x higher LTV than one-time buyers
- The critical moment: Month 2-3. If they survive this, retention stabilises.
- Win-back cost for lapsed subscribers is 1/5 of new acquisition cost
- **Key metric:** Monthly churn rate. Below 5% = excellent. Above 10% = crisis.

## IV. THE DISPROVEN LIST (Don't Repeat These)

These are common "best practices" that data consistently shows DON'T work for supplement DTC:
- ❌ Discount-led acquisition (trains customers to wait for sales, destroys margins)
- ❌ Feature-heavy copy (nobody cares about mg counts — they care about OUTCOMES)
- ❌ Stock photography (kills trust instantly in health products)
- ❌ "One campaign fits all" (different awareness stages need different creative)
- ❌ Ignoring email for paid (email has 10x the ROI of paid media)
- ❌ Broad targeting with no creative testing (creative IS the targeting now)
