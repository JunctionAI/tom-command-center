# DBH Competitor Landscape — US & Global Supplement Market
## Last Updated: March 2, 2026

## WHY THIS MATTERS
DBH competes for the same customer attention and ingredient credibility as major US/global
supplement brands. Understanding their strategies helps Meridian:
- Identify positioning gaps DBH can exploit
- Spot influencer/creator trends before they hit NZ
- Benchmark pricing, offers, and creative approaches
- Find ingredients/claims competitors are investing in

## TIER 1 — MEGA BRANDS (>$100M revenue, massive influencer spend)

### AG1 (Athletic Greens)
- Category: Daily greens/all-in-one nutrition
- Revenue: ~$600M+ (2024 est.)
- Strategy: Premium positioning ($99/month subscription), podcast sponsorships
- Influencer model: Long-term ambassador deals (Huberman, Lex Fridman, Tim Ferriss)
- Key ingredients: 75-ingredient blend, probiotics, adaptogens, vitamins
- DBH overlap: Greens category, gut health, immune support
- What to watch: Their subscription model, creator-led content, "one product" simplicity
- Weakness: Expensive, proprietary blend (undisclosed doses), US-centric

### Momentous
- Category: Performance supplements (NSF certified)
- Revenue: ~$100M+
- Strategy: Science-first, partnered with Huberman Lab podcast
- Influencer model: Expert-led (scientists, researchers, elite athletes)
- Key ingredients: Omega-3, creatine, magnesium, collagen, tongkat ali
- DBH overlap: Omega-3, magnesium, collagen, joint health
- What to watch: Their "protocol stacking" approach (multiple SKUs per routine)
- Weakness: Premium pricing, US-only, narrow audience (biohackers)

### Thorne
- Category: Clinical-grade supplements
- Revenue: ~$350M+
- Strategy: Practitioner channel + DTC, Mayo Clinic partnership
- Key ingredients: Fish oil, magnesium, curcumin, collagen, probiotics
- DBH overlap: Fish oil/omega-3, joint health, anti-aging
- What to watch: Their clinical credibility positioning
- Weakness: Clinical branding = less emotional connection

### Garden of Life
- Category: Organic/whole food supplements
- Revenue: ~$500M+ (Nestle-owned)
- Strategy: Clean label, organic certification, Whole Foods distribution
- Key ingredients: Probiotics, collagen, multivitamins, protein
- DBH overlap: Probiotics, collagen, general wellness
- What to watch: Clean label trend, organic positioning

## KEY COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE PATTERNS

### Influencer Trends (US market, relevant for DBH)
1. Long-form podcast sponsorships > short social posts (AG1, Momentous)
2. "Protocol stacking" — creators recommend multiple products as a daily routine
3. TikTok Shop integration — ARMRA, others selling directly in-feed
4. UGC at scale — brands running 50-100+ creators simultaneously
5. Science communicators > lifestyle influencers for premium brands

### Ingredient Trends
1. Colostrum (ARMRA driving awareness)
2. NMN / NAD+ (anti-aging, David Sinclair influence)
3. Magnesium (L-threonate specifically for sleep/cognitive)
4. Adaptogens (ashwagandha, lion's mane)
5. Green Lipped Mussel — DBH's UNIQUE advantage, no major US competitor owns this

### DBH Competitive Advantages
1. NZ sourcing story (clean, pure, end-of-the-world positioning)
2. Green Lipped Mussel expertise (no US brand owns this ingredient)
3. Price advantage vs US premium brands (AG1=$99, Momentous=$50-60/SKU)
4. Real customer data + proven email formulas (48% open rates, 7.78x ROAS)
5. Agility — can outmaneuver Nestle-owned brands on speed to market

### DBH Competitive Weaknesses
1. No influencer programme (vs AG1, ARMRA doing massive creator campaigns)
2. Limited US market presence
3. No subscription model (vs AG1, Seed, Ritual all subscription-first)
4. Brand awareness gap (NZ brand vs US household names)
