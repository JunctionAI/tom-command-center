## DAILY BRAIN RECOVERY ACTIONS (rotate through check-ins)

### 1. COGNITIVE TRAINING (15-20 min/day, Phase 2+)

**Dual N-Back training:**
- Free apps: "Dual N-Back" or "Brain Workshop"
- Start at 2-back, progress when scoring 80%+
- Evidence: improves working memory, fluid intelligence
- 15 minutes daily, 5 days/week
- Track: n-back level and accuracy weekly

**Memory challenges:**
- Memorize a phone number each week (write down, recall without looking throughout day)
- Name 3 things you're grateful for from memory each evening (hippocampal exercise)
- Recall details from conversations earlier in the day (active retrieval practice)

**Word-finding & articulation:**
- Read aloud for 10 minutes daily (any book — forces word retrieval + articulation)
- Voice memos: describe your day for 2 minutes without pausing or saying "um" (practice)
- Learn one new word per day (and use it in conversation)

**Focus training:**
- 25-minute Pomodoro blocks: single task, no phone, no switching
- Start with 1 block daily, build to 4 blocks
- Post-exercise is the best time for focus work (prefrontal cortex is primed)

### 2. BREATHWORK (5 min daily)

**Box breathing (autonomic balance):**
- Inhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds → Exhale 4 seconds → Hold 4 seconds
- 5 minutes, eyes closed
- Resets autonomic nervous system, reduces anxiety
- Best: morning after walk, before cognitive work

**Physiological sigh (acute stress relief):**
- Double inhale through nose (short + long) → extended exhale through mouth
- Repeat 3 times
- Fastest way to calm sympathetic activation (Huberman research)
- Use when anxiety spikes, before social situations, before sleep

**Tyler's breathing has already shifted post-mushroom experience (chest → diaphragmatic).** Don't overcomplicating breathing exercises — his body found the pattern. Just support it with structured practice.

### 3. COLD EXPOSURE (see exercise-protocol.md)

Brain benefits:
- Norepinephrine increase 200-300% (focus, alertness)
- Dopamine increase 250% sustained for hours (ADHD management)
- BDNF increase (neuroplasticity)
- Autonomic flexibility training (HRV improvement)

### 4. SAUNA (Tyler already doing this)

Brain benefits:
- Heat shock proteins (cellular repair)
- BDNF increase (especially post-exercise)
- Growth hormone spike (recovery)
- Parasympathetic activation (relaxation, HRV)
- Blood flow increase (cerebral perfusion)

### 5. NSDR / YOGA NIDRA (10-20 min, afternoon)

- Guided relaxation while awake
- Dopamine restoration in basal ganglia (~65% increase)
- Restores mental energy without sleep
- Perfect for Tyler's afternoon energy dips
- Search: "Huberman NSDR" on YouTube for free protocols
- Do this instead of reaching for phone/screens during low energy

- 25-min Pomodoro blocks with phone in another room
- Written daily plan (Forge morning check-in serves this purpose)

**Dopamine regulation:**
- Avoid dopamine spikes from screens/social media (scrolling = junk dopamine)
- Delay gratification: do the hard task first, reward after

- Cold exposure provides sustained dopamine (not a spike-and-crash like substances)
- Social connection provides dopamine through a healthy pathway

**When focus fails:**

- Bilateral stimulation: cross-body movements (cross crawls, alternating taps)
- 5 minutes of vigorous exercise (jumping jacks, burpees) → return to task
- Physiological sigh (3 double-inhale + long exhales)

## SUBSTANCE CRAVING MANAGEMENT

**Normalized timeline for 7-year daily cannabis users:**
- Weeks 1-4: acute withdrawal (insomnia, irritability, appetite changes, vivid dreams)
- Months 1-3: dopamine receptor upregulation (cravings, anhedonia, mood swings)
- Months 3-6: gradual normalization (cravings reduce, mood stabilizes, sleep improves)
- Months 6-18: full receptor density recovery (brain feels "normal" without substances)

Tyler is at ~8 months. He's past the worst but still in the recalibration window. Cravings may still appear, especially under stress.

**When cravings hit:**
- Acknowledge it: "My brain is asking for dopamine it used to get easily. That's normal."
- Replace it: cold shower, exercise, call a friend, go outside
- Time it: cravings peak and pass within 15-20 minutes. Ride it out.
- Track it: log craving intensity in evening check-in (none / mild / moderate / strong)
- Don't moralize: a craving is not failure. Acting on it isn't even failure. It's data.

## PSYCHEDELIC INTEGRATION (ongoing)

Tyler's 1.7g mushroom experience was therapeutic and significant. Integration principles:

**What happened (scientifically):**
- Psilocybin disrupted the Default Mode Network (DMN) — the brain's "autopilot" that maintains habitual patterns
- This allowed stored somatic trauma to release (the shaking, gagging, primal sounds = fight/flight responses completing)
- The autonomic nervous system reset (HRV jump, breathing shift) was the physiological evidence
- New neural pathways formed in the temporary state of neuroplasticity psilocybin creates

**Integration practices:**
- Journal about the experience: what came up, what changed, what questions remain
- Body awareness: notice when spontaneous releases happen (sighs, yawns, micro-tremors) — these are ongoing integration
- Don't chase the experience: the benefits unfold over weeks/months, not just during the trip
- Maintain the intentions set before the trip: sobriety, trust, letting go, openness
- If considering another session: wait minimum 4-6 weeks, set clear intentions, have support

**IMPORTANT: Psilocybin + recent lithium use**
Tyler stopped lithium ~1 month ago. Lithium and psychedelics can interact (serotonin syndrome risk, seizure risk). The standard recommendation is to be off lithium for minimum 2-4 weeks before any psychedelic use. Tyler's recent 1.7g session appears to have gone well, but this should be flagged for future sessions. Consult Dr. Robin Kelly before any further psychedelic experiences.
