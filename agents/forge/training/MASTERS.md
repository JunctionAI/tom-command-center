## 2. DR. ANDREW HUBERMAN — Neuroscience Protocols

**Core principle:** The nervous system is a controllable machine. Specific protocols (light exposure, cold, breathwork, exercise timing) optimise neurotransmitter function, sleep architecture, and focus.

**Key protocols for Tyler:**

**Morning sunlight (10-30 min within first hour):**
- Sets circadian rhythm, cortisol timing, dopamine baseline
- Tyler already walks at sunrise — PERFECT. Keep this.
- This is the single most impactful free intervention for sleep quality and mood

**Cold exposure (post-exercise or morning):**
- Cold shower (1-3 min) or cold plunge
- Increases norepinephrine 200-300% and dopamine 250% (sustained for hours)
- This is Tyler's replacement dopamine hit — critical for ADHD management without meds
- Protocol: start 30 seconds cold at end of shower, build to 1-3 min over weeks
- DO cold exposure separate from sauna for maximum benefit (not alternating)

**Non-sleep deep rest (NSDR) / Yoga Nidra:**
- 10-20 minute guided relaxation
- Increases dopamine in the basal ganglia by ~65%
- Restores mental energy without sleep
- Perfect for Tyler's afternoon energy dips
- Free: Huberman's NSDR on YouTube

**Exercise timing for brain:**
- Strength training in morning or early afternoon (testosterone + growth hormone peak)
- Zone 2 cardio later if doing two-a-days
- Post-exercise sauna: 20 min at 80-100°C (growth hormone spike, BDNF increase)
- Tyler already does sauna — ensure it's post-exercise for maximum benefit

**ADHD-specific:**
- 40mg caffeine early morning (not more — Tyler's nervous system is still recovering)
- Cold exposure for sustained dopamine elevation
- "Focus blocks": 90-minute work periods with 15-min breaks (ultradian rhythm)
- White noise or binaural beats during focus tasks
- Physical movement before cognitive work (primes prefrontal cortex)

**Tyler's sleep issue: Low REM (max 2 hours of 8):**

**Why REM may be suppressed:**

- Post-concussion: sleep architecture disruption is common for 6-12 months

- Room temperature 18-19°C (cooler room promotes REM)

- Magnesium glycinate before bed (300-400mg — Tyler already doing this, increase to 400)
- Avoid alcohol (completely — Tyler is sober, but even 1-2 drinks crush REM)
- Morning sunlight (sets the circadian clock that times REM cycles)
- REM rebounds naturally as cannabis exits the system — patience required

2. **Replace:** Digestive enzymes if needed, HCl if low stomach acid (ibuprofen history suggests possible)

5. **Rebalance:** Stress management (gut is stress-responsive), sleep, regular eating schedule

**Left-sided gut pain:** If persistent, needs investigation (could be: adhesions from hernia surgery, diverticular issue, muscle strain, stress-related visceral hypersensitivity). Flag if not improving in 4 weeks.
