## THE MASTER PRINCIPLE
Power in the modern era is not held by governments. Governments are administered by elected officials whose tenure is 3-5 years. Real power is held by institutions, capital allocators, and coordination networks whose time horizons are decades. The key to understanding modern power: **who writes the rules that elected officials must operate within.**

The mechanism is always the same:
1. Capital accumulated during one era
2. Deployed into institutions (think tanks, universities, media, foundations)
3. Institutions produce "consensus" — the range of acceptable policy
4. Politicians operate within that consensus or lose funding/access
5. Capital preserved and compounded across generations

### The Narrative Mechanism
Journalists don't receive memos to suppress stories. The mechanism is subtler:
- Editorial decisions flow from who owns the publication
- Owners sit on boards of companies that would be affected by certain coverage
- Editors who publish problematic stories don't advance
- Sources are cultivated over years — officials who leak critical stories lose access
- Result: self-censorship. Not orders from above. Internalized understanding of what's acceptable.

- Revealed: HSBC moved money for drug cartels after being fined for same. Deutsche Bank processed $1.3T in questionable transactions. JPMorgan, Standard Chartered, Barclays flagged for moving dirty money.
- Key: banks file SARs (suspicious activity reports) to FinCEN but continue processing the transactions. Filing the report protects them legally while the money moves.

### The LIBOR Scandal (2012)
- LIBOR: the benchmark interest rate used to price ~$300 trillion in financial contracts globally
- 16 major banks were collusively manipulating the rate daily
- Barclays, UBS, Deutsche Bank, Rabobank, RBS all pleaded guilty or reached settlements
- Total fines: ~$9 billion
- Criminal convictions: a handful of junior traders. No senior executives prosecuted.
- Mechanism: traders literally emailing each other asking to move the rate, got it done, profited on derivatives positions
- Key: this was not individual wrongdoing — it was institutional. And no institution was shut down.
