## BUSINESS MODEL
ASE Technology Holding Co., Ltd. (ASX) generates revenue primarily through its Assembly, Testing, and Material (ATM) segment (61.4% of Q2 2025 net revenues at NT$92,565 million, up 19.0% YoY) and Electronic Manufacturing Services (EMS) segment (down 6.6% YoY to NT$58,770 million in Q2 2025).[1][2] Customers include chip designers and integrated device manufacturers (IDMs) who outsource back-end processes like packaging bare silicon wafers into finished, tested chips, plus broader electronics firms for EMS like telecommunication motherboards and automotive/industrial products.[2] Switching costs are high due to capital-intensive specialized equipment, long-term contracts, and rigorous qualification processes for packaging/testing quality.[2] Revenue streams break down as packaging (46% of total in 1Q25, core of ATM's 17.3% YoY growth), testing (11%), and EMS (42%, up 4.9% YoY in 1Q25); ATM offers higher margins (raw materials 27% of revenue) vs. EMS (79%).[2]

## COMPETITIVE POSITION (Porter's Five Forces)
**Threat of new entrants (high barriers):** Low threat due to capital-intensive nature (Q2 2025 CapEx US$992 million on AI platforms like Co-Packaged Optics and VIPack™), specialized technology, and scale required for OSAT leadership.[1][2]  
**Supplier power (moderate):** Relies on raw materials and equipment, but low ATM material costs (27% of revenue) mitigate; diversification and liquidity (NT$355,299 million unused credit as of June 2025) reduce vulnerability.[2]  
**Buyer power (high):** Chip designers/IDMs have options among OSATs, pressuring pricing amid competitive industry and demand fluctuations.[3]  
**Threat of substitutes (low-moderate):** In-house IDM packaging possible, but outsourcing trend grows with AI complexity; no direct substitutes for advanced packaging/testing.[1]  
**Competitive rivalry (high):** Intense in OSAT/EMS with global players; ASE leads as world's largest OSAT via scale and AI investments.[2]  
**Sources of advantage:** Scale in ATM (TTM revenue $19.672 billion ending Sep 2025, up 4.06% YoY), advanced packaging share (46% of ATM in Apr 2025), and AI-focused CapEx; disadvantage in EMS margin squeeze from high material costs.[1][2]
