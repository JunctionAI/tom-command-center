## 2. Andrew Huberman — Neuroscience Protocols

**Core principle:** Actionable neuroscience. Specific protocols with specific mechanisms.

**Key protocols for Tom:**
- Morning sunlight exposure (10-20min): Sets circadian rhythm, cortisol peak timing. Critical for sleep normalisation.
- Cold exposure (1-3min cold shower): 2.5x dopamine increase lasting 3+ hours. Natural, sustainable dopamine without downregulation. Perfect counter to substance-seeking.
- Non-sleep deep rest (NSDR/Yoga Nidra): 10-20min. Replenishes dopamine, improves learning consolidation. Tom's afternoon fatigue → NSDR instead of full sleep.
- Deliberate focus training: 90-minute focus blocks with ultradian rhythm. Matches Tom's hyperfocus tendency but structures it.
- Dopamine scheduling: Front-load hardest cognitive work in first 8 hours after waking (peak dopamine and cortisol).

**On cannabis recovery:**
- CB1 receptor density normalises by ~28 days of abstinence
- Full endocannabinoid system recalibration: 60-90 days
- Expect: vivid dreams returning (good sign — REM rebound), temporary anxiety increase (GABAergic rebound), improved memory formation

## 3. Jeff Volek & Stuart Phillips — Exercise Science

**Muscle protein synthesis:**
- 0.4g/kg protein per meal, 4 meals = optimal daily distribution
- Leucine threshold: 2.5-3g per meal triggers maximal MPS
- Post-workout window: less critical than once thought, but 20-40g protein within 2hrs is optimal
- Creatine 5g/day: proven cognitive AND physical performance enhancer

**Training for brain + body simultaneously:**
- Resistance training increases IGF-1 → neuroplasticity
- Zone 2 cardio increases BDNF → neurogenesis (new brain cells in hippocampus)
- HIIT releases growth hormone → brain repair + fat loss
- Combination of all three in weekly program = maximum neural benefit

## 4. Matthew Walker — Sleep Science

**Core principle:** Sleep is the single most effective thing you can do for brain health. Period.

**Key mechanisms:**
- Glymphatic system: Brain's waste clearance system operates during deep sleep. Clears beta-amyloid, tau, and metabolic waste. One night of poor sleep = measurable accumulation.
- Memory consolidation: Hippocampus → Cortex transfer during sleep spindles. Cannabis suppresses this transfer.
- Emotional regulation: REM sleep processes emotional memories. Chronic sleep debt → increased anxiety, irritability.
- Prefrontal recovery: The last brain region to develop (not fully until ~25) and first to degrade from sleep debt.

**Tom's sleep protocol:**
- Consistent sleep window (same time ±30min, 7 days/week)
- No caffeine after 2pm (half-life: 6 hours)
- Screen dimming/blue light blocking after sunset
- Cool bedroom (18-19°C optimal)
- Magnesium glycinate 400mg 1hr before bed
- If 4am bedtime pattern returns: it takes ~1 week of consistent earlier bedtime to shift circadian rhythm. Move by 30min every 2 days.

## 5. Anna Lembke — Dopamine Nation

**Core principle:** Pleasure and pain are co-located in the brain. Every pleasure has an equal and opposite pain response (opponent-process theory).

**The dopamine balance for Tom:**
- Chronic substance use tilts the balance toward pain (tolerance)
- Abstinence causes temporary pain spike (withdrawal/craving)
- 30-day reset allows balance to restore to baseline
- After reset: normal pleasures (food, exercise, social connection, achievement) feel rewarding again

**Practical application:**
- The first 2 weeks are hardest (Tom has passed this for weed)
- Weeks 3-4: cravings decrease but can spike with triggers
- Weeks 5-8: noticeable improvement in baseline mood and motivation
- The porn cycle follows the same neuroscience — tolerance, escalation, real-intimacy difficulty
- Exercise is the most effective natural dopamine intervention that DOESN'T cause downregulation

## 6. Rhonda Patrick — Nutritional Biochemistry

**Brain-specific nutrition mechanisms:**
- DHA (omega-3): structural component of neuronal membranes. Deficiency = stiff membranes = poor signalling. 2-4g fish oil/day is therapeutic dose.
- Sulforaphane (broccoli sprouts): activates Nrf2 pathway → clears oxidative stress → reduces neuroinflammation from head impacts
- Vitamin D: neurosteroid that regulates 200+ genes. NZ winter + indoor work = near-certain deficiency. 4000 IU minimum.
- Magnesium: cofactor in 300+ enzymatic reactions including neurotransmitter synthesis. Most people are deficient. Glycinate form for brain + sleep.
- Creatine: not just for muscles. Brain cells use creatine phosphate for rapid energy. 5g/day improves working memory and cognitive performance.
