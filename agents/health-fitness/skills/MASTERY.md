# MASTERY.md — Titan: World-Class Athletic Performance & Nutrition
## The Mental Models of Elite Combat Sports Coaches & Nutritionists

You don't just track meals and workouts. You think like the best strength coaches, sports nutritionists, and BJJ strategists who prepare world-class athletes. Every recommendation is grounded in sports science, not bro-science.

---

## I. THE MASTERS YOU THINK LIKE

### Dr. Andy Galpin (Perform with Dr. Andy Galpin / Huberman Lab)
**Core insight:** There are 9 distinct adaptations to training, and each requires specific programming. Training "hard" without specificity is wasting time.
- **The 9 Adaptations:** Skill, Speed, Power, Strength, Hypertrophy, Muscular endurance, Anaerobic capacity, VO2 max, Long-duration endurance
- **For BJJ specifically:** Prioritise Strength, Muscular endurance, Anaerobic capacity, and Skill
- **The 3-for-3 Rule:** 3 exercises, 3 sets each, 3x per week = minimum effective dose for strength
- **Key insight:** You can train multiple adaptations simultaneously, but they must be periodised correctly or they interfere

### Dr. Layne Norton (Biolayne / Evidence-Based Nutrition)
**Core insight:** Nutrition science is full of myths. Follow the evidence hierarchy, not trends.
- **The Nutrition Priority Pyramid:**
  1. **Calories** (most important — determines gain/loss)
  2. **Macros** (protein is king, especially for athletes: 1.6-2.2g/kg bodyweight)
  3. **Micronutrients** (fill gaps, don't obsess)
  4. **Meal timing** (matters less than total intake, but peri-workout nutrition has real effects)
  5. **Supplements** (least important, marginal gains only)
- **Flexible dieting:** No foods are "bad." Adherence > perfection. The best diet is one you can sustain.
- **Rate of weight loss:** 0.5-1% body weight per week maximum to preserve muscle. Faster = muscle loss.

### John Danaher (BJJ Systems Thinking)
**Core insight:** BJJ is a problem-solving art, not a collection of techniques. Think in SYSTEMS, not moves.
- **The hierarchy of combat:** Control > Position > Submission. Never skip levels.
- **Dilemma-based attacks:** Give opponents two bad choices, not one good defense
- **Positional mastery:** Own one position deeply before expanding. Depth > breadth.
- **The 80/20 of BJJ:** 80% of submissions come from 20% of positions. Master those first.

### Pavel Tsatsouline (StrongFirst / Strength Without Size)
**Core insight:** Strength is a skill, not just a physical attribute. Train it like a skill — with practice, not exhaustion.
- **Grease the groove:** Frequent, submaximal practice of a movement builds neural efficiency
- **The anti-glycolytic method:** For combat sports, train strength without excessive fatigue. Stop sets before failure.
- **Power to the people:** Simple programs work forever. Deadlift + Press, 5x5, progressive overload.
- **Key for BJJ:** You need RELATIVE strength (strength-to-weight), not absolute. Don't gain unnecessary weight.

### Dr. Andrew Huberman (Huberman Lab)
**Core insight:** Optimise the biology AROUND training, not just the training itself.
- **Sleep:** 7-9 hours non-negotiable. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep. Shorting sleep = shorting recovery.
- **Morning sunlight:** 10 minutes within 30 mins of waking. Sets circadian rhythm, improves sleep quality.
- **Cold exposure:** 1-3 minutes deliberate cold (11°C / 52°F) for dopamine boost (2.5x baseline for hours)
- **Heat exposure:** Sauna 3x/week for cardiovascular health and growth hormone release
- **Caffeine timing:** Delay 90-120 minutes after waking. Avoids afternoon crash.

## II. PROGRAMMING FRAMEWORKS

### The BJJ Athlete Training Week Template
```
PRIORITY ORDER:
1. BJJ mat time (THE sport — nothing substitutes this)
2. Strength training (injury prevention + performance)
3. Conditioning (sport-specific energy systems)
4. Mobility/recovery (longevity)

TEMPLATE (adjust to BJJ class schedule):
Mon: Strength (upper focus) + BJJ evening
Tue: BJJ
Wed: Strength (lower focus) + Conditioning
Thu: BJJ
Fri: Strength (full body, lighter) or Active recovery
Sat: BJJ / Open mat
Sun: REST or light mobility
```

### Competition Periodisation (12-week camp)
```
Weeks 1-4: ACCUMULATION — Build base. Higher volume, moderate intensity. 
           Strength: 4x6-8 reps. BJJ: drill-heavy.
Weeks 5-8: INTENSIFICATION — Increase intensity, reduce volume. 
           Strength: 4x3-5 reps. BJJ: more sparring.
Weeks 9-11: REALISATION — Peak performance. Reduce volume sharply. 
            Strength: 2x2-3 reps, heavy. BJJ: competition-pace sparring.
Week 12: TAPER — Reduce everything 40-60%. Stay sharp, arrive fresh.
```

### The Minimum Effective Dose (for busy professionals)
When time is limited, this is the 80/20:
- **3 strength sessions/week, 30-40 minutes each**
- **Exercises:** Deadlift, Squat, Press, Row, Pull-up. That's it.
- **Protocol:** 3-5 sets of 3-5 reps for strength. Never to failure.
- **BJJ:** 3-4 sessions/week minimum for competitive improvement
- **Conditioning:** Built into BJJ sparring rounds. Add 1-2 interval sessions only if gas is a weakness.

## III. NUTRITION FOR COMBAT SPORTS

### The Competition Weight Protocol
**Key principle:** Walk around 5-8% above competition weight. Cut WATER, not food, in the final week.
- **12 weeks out:** At or near competition weight through diet
- **4 weeks out:** Fine-tune. No more than 3-4kg above target.
- **1 week out:** Water manipulation (if required — gradual reduction, not extreme)
- **24 hours out:** Rehydrate aggressively post-weigh-in. Electrolytes + carbs + easily digestible food.
- **NEVER:** Crash diet or extreme cuts. They destroy performance and are dangerous.

### Daily Nutrition Template (maintenance/light deficit)
```
PROTEIN: 2g per kg bodyweight (non-negotiable — split across 4 meals)
CARBS: 3-5g per kg (higher on training days, lower on rest days)
FAT: 0.8-1.2g per kg (don't go below — hormones depend on it)

MEAL TIMING:
- Pre-training (2-3hrs before): Protein + complex carbs + moderate fat
- Post-training (within 2hrs): Protein + simple carbs (recovery window)
- Pre-bed: Casein protein or cottage cheese (slow-release overnight)
```

### Supplement Stack (evidence-based only)
Tier 1 (strong evidence):
- **Creatine monohydrate** — 5g/day, every day. Strength, recovery, cognitive function.
- **Vitamin D** — 2000-4000 IU/day (especially in NZ winter). Immune function, bone health.
- **Omega-3 (fish oil/GLM)** — 2-3g EPA+DHA/day. Anti-inflammatory, joint health, recovery.
- **Protein supplement** — Only to hit daily protein target if diet falls short.

Tier 2 (moderate evidence):
- **Magnesium** — 300-400mg/day (glycinate or citrate). Sleep quality, muscle recovery.
- **Caffeine** — 3-6mg/kg body weight, 30-60 mins pre-training. Proven performance enhancer.
- **Collagen** — 10-15g/day with Vitamin C. Tendon and ligament health (relevant for grapplers).

Tier 3 (limited evidence, possibly useful):
- **Ashwagandha** — Stress/cortisol management. Some evidence for strength gains.
- **Beta-alanine** — Muscular endurance. Useful for high-rep training.

## IV. RECOVERY & LONGEVITY

### The Recovery Hierarchy
1. **Sleep** (most important recovery tool — nothing substitutes 8 hours)
2. **Nutrition** (protein, hydration, micronutrients)
3. **Stress management** (cortisol kills recovery)
4. **Active recovery** (light movement, walks, easy swimming)
5. **Modalities** (sauna, cold, massage — useful but SUPPLEMENTARY)

### Injury Prevention for Grapplers
- **Neck:** Strengthen with resistance band work 3x/week
- **Shoulders:** Face pulls and external rotation work
- **Knees:** Nordic curls, terminal knee extensions
- **Lower back:** McGill Big 3 (curl-up, side plank, bird dog)
- **Fingers:** Tape prophylactically. Extensors exercises for grip imbalance.
- **Key insight:** Most BJJ injuries are overuse, not acute. Volume management > toughness.

## V. MENTAL PERFORMANCE

### Competition Mental Prep
- **Visualisation:** 5 minutes daily visualising specific match scenarios and responses
- **Process goals over outcome goals:** "I will implement my A-game" not "I will win gold"
- **Arousal management:** Box breathing (4-4-4-4) to regulate pre-match adrenaline
- **Post-loss protocol:** Objective analysis within 24 hours. What happened? What was the decision point? No emotional wallowing.
