## IMPORTANT CONTEXT

Jackson has POTS (Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome) with severe deconditioning after 3+ months bedbound. The Levine/Dallas Protocol showed 53% of POTS patients no longer met diagnostic criteria after 3 months of structured exercise.

However, the standard protocol is often TOO demanding for severely deconditioned patients. This is a MODIFIED version that starts lower and progresses slower.

**Do NOT start this until Phase 3.** Phases 1-2 focus on nervous system regulation, nutrition, and baseline stabilisation. Jumping to exercise too early risks a crash that sets back progress and reinforces the fear-symptom cycle.

---

## PRE-EXERCISE FOUNDATIONS (Phase 2, before exercise begins)

**Hydration baseline:**
- 2-3 litres of fluid per day (water, electrolyte drinks, herbal tea)
- Start tracking daily fluid intake

**Salt loading:**
- 2-3g extra salt per day (beyond normal diet)
- Add to food/shakes, or use electrolyte tablets
- Salt increases blood volume, which directly helps POTS

**Compression:**
- Waist-high compression stockings for any upright activity (if tolerable)
- Abdominal binder as alternative

**Standing tolerance baseline:**
- Time how long Jackson can stand comfortably before symptoms spike
- This is the starting data point

---

## MONTH 1: RECUMBENT ONLY (Phase 3 start)

**Goal:** Rebuild cardiovascular capacity without triggering orthostatic stress.

**Exercise types (choose based on access):**
- Recumbent bike (preferred — available at most gyms or affordable home options)
- Swimming (ideal — water pressure acts as compression, horizontal position eliminates orthostatic stress)
- Rowing machine (lying or seated recumbent position)
- Recumbent stepper

**Frequency:** 3 sessions per week (Mon/Wed/Fri or similar, never consecutive days initially)

**Starting duration:** Whatever is tolerable. Literally 5 minutes counts. If 5 minutes is too much, start at 3 minutes.

**Progression:**
- Week 1-2: Find tolerable duration (5-15 min). Low resistance/intensity.
- Week 3-4: Add 2-3 minutes per session if previous duration felt manageable
- Target by end of Month 1: 20-25 minutes per session

**Heart rate target:** Resting HR + 15-25 bpm (NOT percentage of max HR — that's too aggressive for POTS)
- Example: If Jackson's resting HR is 70, target 85-95 during exercise
- Use a HR monitor (smartwatch or chest strap)

**Lower body resistance training (add from Week 2):**
- Leg press (seated/recumbent)
- Seated leg extensions
- Seated calf raises
- Start with body weight or very light resistance
- 2 sets of 8-10 reps
- Lower body muscle mass improves venous return (blood pumping back to heart), which directly addresses POTS

**STOP SIGNS — end the session if:**
- Heart rate exceeds resting + 40 bpm
- Lightheadedness that doesn't resolve after sitting/lying for 2 minutes
- Nausea that persists
- Chest pain
- Vision blackout or severe blurring

**Post-exercise:**
- Lie or sit for 5-10 minutes
- Hydrate (500ml minimum)
- Track: duration, average HR, symptoms during/after, how he felt 2 hours later

## MONTH 2: SEMI-UPRIGHT (Phase 3 continued)

**Goal:** Begin introducing upright positions while maintaining recumbent base.

**Exercise mix:**
- 2 sessions recumbent (bike/swimming/rowing) — maintain base
- 1-2 sessions semi-upright:
  - Upright stationary bike
  - Walking (start on flat surfaces, 10-15 minutes)
  - Elliptical (handles provide stability)

**Progression:**
- Week 5-6: Add 1 semi-upright session per week alongside 2 recumbent
- Week 7-8: Move to 2 semi-upright + 2 recumbent if tolerated
- Walking: start at 10 min, add 2-3 min per week

**Heart rate target:** Resting + 20-30 bpm

**Lower body resistance:** Progress to 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Slightly increase resistance.

**POTS management during upright exercise:**
- Wear compression stockings
- Hydrate before, during, and after
- Have somewhere to sit immediately if symptoms spike
- Walking: plan routes with benches/rest spots

---

## MONTH 3: UPRIGHT (Phase 4)

**Goal:** Transition to normal upright exercise patterns.

**Exercise mix:**
- 1-2 sessions recumbent (maintenance)
- 2-3 sessions upright:
  - Walking 20-30 minutes
  - Light jogging intervals (30 seconds jog / 2 minutes walk)
  - Bodyweight exercises (squats, lunges — with support available)
  - Upright bike or elliptical 25-35 minutes

**Frequency:** 4-5 sessions per week

**Heart rate target:** Resting + 25-35 bpm

**Lower body resistance:** Full gym routine if accessible. Focus on legs — this is the POTS medicine.

## TRACKING (all months)

After every exercise session, log:
- Exercise type and duration
- Average and peak heart rate
- Pre-exercise standing HR (measure after 3 min standing)
- Post-exercise symptoms (0-10)
- Energy level 2 hours after (0-10)
- Any crashes next day (Y/N)

This data feeds pattern detection. The agent will track:
- Standing HR delta trend (should decrease over weeks)
- Post-exercise energy trend (should stabilise then improve)
- Crash frequency (should decrease)

---

## CRASH PROTOCOL

If Jackson crashes after exercise (severe fatigue, symptoms spike for 24+ hours):
1. Rest. Do not exercise for 2-3 days.
2. Return at 50% of the duration/intensity that caused the crash
3. Progress more slowly (add 1 minute per session instead of 2-3)
4. This is NOT failure — it's data. The nervous system found the edge; now we know where it is.
5. Frame it: "Your body told you where the limit is right now. That's useful information, not a setback."
