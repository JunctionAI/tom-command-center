## CONTEXT

Jackson is 183cm, 55-56kg — severely underweight (BMI ~16.5, healthy range 18.5-24.9). Progressive weight loss since 2022, stabilised early 2024. Eating is a major stressor due to:
- Dysphagia (swallowing muscle weakness)
- Chewing/swallowing itself triggers tension and symptom spikes
- Decisions around food are a source of stress and self-attack
- He has committed to liquid foods to reduce this stress

The strategy: LIQUID FIRST. Shakes and smoothies are the foundation. Solid foods are bonus, never required.

## CALORIE TARGETS BY PHASE

**Phase 1 (Foundation):** 1500-1800 kcal/day
- Focus: establish any consistent caloric intake
- 3-4 shakes per day is enough
- Don't overwhelm with targets — any improvement over current intake is a win

**Phase 2 (Nervous System Regulation):** 1800-2200 kcal/day
- Add calorie-dense ingredients to existing shakes
- Introduce supplements
- Begin optional soft foods (yoghurt, soup, mashed potato)

**Phase 3 (Physical Rehabilitation):** 2200-2500 kcal/day
- Exercise increases caloric needs
- Add extra shake on exercise days
- Transition some meals to soft foods if tolerated

**Phase 4-5 (Expanding/Independence):** 2500-3000 kcal/day
- Target weight gain: 0.5-1kg per week until 60kg+
- Mix of shakes, soft foods, and normal meals as tolerated
- Maintain shake routine alongside meals for calorie insurance

## HIGH-CALORIE SHAKE RECIPES (600-800 kcal each)

**The Foundation Shake (700 kcal)**
- 400ml whole milk (or oat milk if dairy intolerant)
- 1 banana
- 2 tbsp peanut butter (or almond butter)
- 1 scoop protein powder (whey or plant-based, ~25g protein)
- 1 tbsp MCT oil or coconut oil
- Blend smooth

**The Recovery Shake (750 kcal)**
- 400ml whole milk
- 1/2 cup oats (blend first into flour for smoothness)
- 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
- 2 tbsp nut butter
- 1 scoop protein powder
- 1 tbsp flax oil (omega-3 source)
- Handful frozen berries

**The Simple High-Cal (600 kcal)**
- 400ml whole milk
- 2 tbsp nut butter
- 1 banana
- 1 tbsp honey
- Blend and drink

**The Evening Wind-Down (500 kcal)**
- 400ml warm milk (or oat milk)
- 1 tbsp honey
- 1 tbsp nut butter
- 1 tsp cacao powder
- Pinch of cinnamon
- Warm and sip (not blended — more like a hot chocolate)

**Tips:**
- Make shakes cold for best palatability
- Sip throughout the hour rather than chugging
- If full shakes feel too much, make them smaller and have more frequently
- Pre-blend and refrigerate for convenience (lasts 24 hours)
- MCT oil and nut butters are the calorie multipliers

## SUPPLEMENT STACK

**Priority supplements (start Phase 1):**

- **Magnesium Glycinate:** 400-600mg elemental magnesium daily (split AM/PM)
  - Why: Neuroprotection, muscle relaxation, reduces tension, supports sleep
  - Glycinate form is best absorbed and has calming effect
  - Take with food or shake

- **Omega-3 (Fish Oil or Algae-based):** 2-4g combined EPA/DHA daily
  - Why: Anti-inflammatory, nerve cell membrane repair, brain health
  - Higher dose for anti-inflammatory effect
  - Take with food (reduces fishy burps)

- **Vitamin D3:** 2000-4000 IU daily
  - Why: Neuroprotective, commonly deficient in indoor-bound/underweight individuals
  - Take with fat-containing food for absorption

- **B-Complex:** Daily (or individual B1, B6, B12)
  - Why: Nerve function, energy metabolism, neuropathic symptom support
  - B1 (thiamine) especially important during refeeding
  - B12: supplement if levels suboptimal (ask Dr Kenedi to check)

**Phase 2 additions:**

- **Electrolyte tablets/powder:** For POTS (sodium, potassium, magnesium)
  - Why: Increases blood volume, reduces orthostatic symptoms
  - 2-3g extra sodium per day target
  - Can add to water throughout the day

- **Zinc:** 15-30mg daily
  - Why: Immune function, nerve repair, often depleted in underweight
  - Take with food to avoid nausea

## PRACTICAL STRATEGIES FOR EATING DIFFICULTY

**For dysphagia/chewing stress:**
- Liquids first, always. Shakes are a complete meal.
- If attempting solid food: very soft options (yoghurt, avocado, scrambled eggs, soup, mashed banana)
- Small bites, take time, no rush
- Warm liquids may be easier to swallow than cold for some people (experiment)

**For decision stress around food:**
- Reduce choices: pick 2-3 shake recipes and rotate
- Prepare ingredients in advance (pre-measured bags in fridge)
- Don't make food a project — make it a simple routine
- If a shake is "all you managed today" — that is enough. It is not failure.

**For eating-triggered symptom spikes:**
- Frame it: the neuromuscular effort of eating triggers tension in an already-sensitised nervous system. The act of eating is not causing damage.
- Safety reappraisal before meals: "Eating requires effort from my muscles and nerves. My nervous system may react. That reaction is not damage."
- Liquid meals inherently reduce this trigger
- If symptoms spike after eating: rest, vagal toning (humming or slow exhale), and wait — it will settle

**Tracking:**
- Log estimated daily calories (even rough: "3 shakes = ~2100")
- Log whether supplements taken (Y/N)
- Log fluid intake (target 2-3L)
- Don't track obsessively — a rough daily estimate is enough

## REFEEDING SAFETY NOTE

Jackson's BMI (~16.5) puts him in a category where rapid caloric increase carries a small risk of refeeding syndrome (electrolyte imbalances, particularly phosphorus and potassium). This is most relevant when going from very low intake to suddenly high intake.

**Mitigation:**
- Increase calories gradually (not doubling overnight)
- Take the supplement stack (B vitamins and electrolytes protect against this)
- If Jackson experiences any unusual symptoms when significantly increasing calories (muscle weakness, confusion, heart rhythm changes), encourage him to contact his doctor
- This risk is low with gradual increases and supplementation, but worth being aware of
