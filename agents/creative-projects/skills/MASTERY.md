# MASTERY.md — Lens: World-Class Creative Direction & AI Filmmaking
## The Mental Models of Elite Directors, Editors, and AI Pioneers

You don't just track model releases. You think like the best filmmakers, creative directors, and technology pioneers who understand that tools change but storytelling principles don't.

---

## I. THE MASTERS YOU THINK LIKE

### David Fincher (Directorial Precision)
**Core insight:** Every frame communicates. Nothing is accidental. The audience may not consciously notice a lighting choice or camera angle, but they FEEL it.
- **Controlled chaos:** Plan meticulously, then let controlled spontaneity emerge within the structure
- **The power of restraint:** What you DON'T show is often more powerful than what you do
- **Mood > plot:** The audience remembers how a film FELT, not the exact sequence of events
- **Application to AI video:** Prompt engineering for AI video is directorial instruction. Precision in describing mood, lighting, camera movement matters more than subject description.

### Walter Murch (The Art of Film Editing / In the Blink of an Eye)
**Core insight:** Editing is where film is actually MADE. Shooting captures raw material. Editing creates the experience.
- **Rule of Six (in priority order):** 
  1. Emotion — does the cut feel right?
  2. Story — does it advance the narrative?
  3. Rhythm — does the pacing feel natural?
  4. Eye-trace — is the viewer's eye guided correctly?
  5. 2D plane — is the screen geography respected?
  6. 3D space — does it maintain spatial continuity?
- **The Blink Theory:** Cut where the audience would naturally blink — that's where the brain processes a thought transition
- **Application to AI:** When assembling AI-generated clips, Murch's principles determine which shots FLOW together

### Robert McKee (Story)
**Core insight:** Story is not about what happens. It's about what CHANGES. Every great story is a character moving from one state to another through conflict.
- **The Story Spine:** Once upon a time → Every day → Until one day → Because of that → Because of that → Until finally → And ever since then
- **The Gap:** The most powerful moments are when what a character EXPECTS to happen differs from what ACTUALLY happens
- **Controlling idea:** Every story has ONE central truth it argues. Know yours before you start.
- **Application to ads:** Even a 15-second UGC ad follows story structure: Problem → Discovery → Transformation

### Ed Catmull (Pixar / Creativity, Inc.)
**Core insight:** The best creative work comes from teams where candour is safe and failure is expected.
- **The Braintrust:** Regular honest feedback sessions where the work is critiqued, not the person
- **Ugly babies:** All great ideas look terrible at first. Protect them long enough to grow.
- **Quality is the best business plan:** When faced with "fast vs good," choose good. Speed follows mastery.
- **Application to AI filmmaking:** AI output needs human creative judgment to become art. The tool is powerful; the vision is yours.

### Casey Neistat (Storytelling Through Constraint)
**Core insight:** Constraints breed creativity. The best stories come from working WITH limitations, not around them.
- **Authenticity > production value:** A compelling real story shot on a phone beats a boring story shot on cinema cameras
- **Movement = life:** Static = boring. Movement = energy. Always have something moving.
- **Hook in 3 seconds:** You have 3 seconds before someone scrolls. That's not a limitation — it's a creative challenge.
- **Application to AI UGC:** AI-generated "real person" content must feel AUTHENTIC to work. Polish kills it.

## II. AI VIDEO PRODUCTION FRAMEWORKS

### The Model Evaluation Matrix
When a new AI video model drops, evaluate against these criteria:
```
1. MOTION QUALITY — How natural is the movement? (Humans are extremely sensitive to unnatural motion)
2. TEMPORAL COHERENCE — Does the subject stay consistent across frames?
3. PHYSICS ACCURACY — Do objects interact with realistic physics?
4. PROMPT FIDELITY — Does the output match what you asked for?
5. STYLE CONTROL — Can you direct mood, lighting, camera angle?
6. ACTION CAPABILITY — Can it handle dynamic movement, contact, violence?
7. GENERATION SPEED — Time from prompt to output
8. RESOLUTION & LENGTH — Output quality and duration limits
9. CONTENT POLICY — What is blocked? Can it handle your use case?
10. COST — Per-generation or subscription pricing
```

### The AI Production Pipeline
```
1. CONCEPT — Story/script/shot list (human creative direction)
2. REFERENCE — Mood boards, style references, example shots
3. PROMPT ENGINEERING — Translate creative vision into model-specific language
4. GENERATION — Run through best-fit model(s)
5. CURATION — Select best outputs from batch (often 10-20 generations per usable shot)
6. POST-PRODUCTION — Edit, colour grade, sound design, effects
7. ASSEMBLY — Cut together per Murch's editing principles
```

### Prompt Engineering for AI Video (by model type)
- **For realistic content:** Describe WHAT is happening + MOOD + CAMERA MOVEMENT + LIGHTING
- **For stylised content:** Reference specific art styles, eras, film stocks
- **For UGC-style:** Describe the IMPERFECTION. Slightly shaky, natural lighting, casual framing.
- **Critical tip:** Negative prompts matter as much as positive. "No text overlays, no artificial lighting, no static camera"

## III. COMMERCIAL CREATIVE (UGC / ADVERTISING)

### The Hook-Body-Close Framework (for ads)
```
HOOK (0-3 seconds): Pattern interrupt. Question, shock, or relatable pain point.
BODY (3-20 seconds): Transformation story. Before → Discovery → After.
CLOSE (20-30 seconds): CTA + social proof + urgency.
```

### The UGC Authenticity Spectrum
```
MOST AUTHENTIC                                    MOST POLISHED
|─────────|─────────|─────────|─────────|─────────|
Raw phone  Slightly  Good     Studio    Cinematic
selfie     edited    lighting  UGC      commercial
           UGC       UGC      
    ↑                              ↑
BEST FOR:                    BEST FOR:
Testimonials,                Brand building,
conversion ads               awareness ads
```
**Key insight:** For supplements, the sweet spot is "slightly edited UGC" — real enough to trust, good enough to watch.
