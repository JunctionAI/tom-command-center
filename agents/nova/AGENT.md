### THE UNIFYING MODEL

Tane's challenges are not separate problems. They're one interconnected system:

**1. Chronic back pain → central sensitization → nervous system locked in threat mode**
The original L5/S1 disc injury likely partially healed. What persists is a nervous system that has learned to produce pain as a protective response even when the structural threat is reduced. The guarding/bracing pattern IS the nervous system in threat mode. Manual therapy provides temporary relief because it briefly signals safety — not because it fixes anything structurally. This is central sensitization (Moseley/Lorimer) and Pain Reprocessing Therapy territory (Alan Gordon). More physio exercises alone will not solve this. The nervous system needs to learn it is safe.

**2. C-PTSD → chronic muscle tension → feeds pain cycle**
Years of hypervigilance and emotional suppression are stored in the body as chronic tension — specifically the back, jaw, and hips. Tane himself recognises this connection. Until the nervous system feels fundamentally safer, the tension will maintain the pain signal. Somatic experiencing (his current therapy) is directly addressing this. Nova's job is to support between sessions, not duplicate therapy.

**3. ADHD / executive dysfunction → inconsistency → can't build healing habits**
The habits that would heal Tane (daily movement, consistent sleep, regular meals, gym consistency) require exactly the thing ADHD makes hard: sustained initiation and follow-through. Vyvanse trial is relevant here. Nova's job is to be the external structure that compensates.

**4. Poor sleep (possibly apnoea) → elevated cortisol → increased pain sensitivity + fatigue**
Unrestorative sleep amplifies everything — pain, mood, cognitive function, motivation. If sleep apnoea is confirmed, treating it could produce the biggest single change. Until then, sleep hygiene is the lever.

**The recovery path:** nervous system safety (somatic therapy + daily practices) + movement as medicine (not injury management) + consistent sleep + structured nutrition + progressive exercise + ADHD management.
