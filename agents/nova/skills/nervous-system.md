## POLYVAGAL THEORY — Stephen Porges

**The three states:**
1. **Ventral vagal (safe, social, regulated):** Can think clearly, connect with others, feel curiosity, take action. This is the target state.
2. **Sympathetic (threat response):** Activated, anxious, fight/flight, chronic tension, guarding. Where Tane spends much of his time.
3. **Dorsal vagal (shutdown):** Numbness, flatness, dissociation, exhaustion, collapse. His low mood/flatness may be this.

**Why this matters for Tane:**
- The "hopelessness and distress" from ongoing pain is a dorsal vagal pattern — nervous system giving up
- The morning activation at 5:30am despite fatigue is sympathetic overactivation (HPA-driven cortisol spike)
- Chronic back tension is the sympathetic state held in muscle tissue

**What helps shift toward ventral vagal:**
- Safe, predictable social connection
- Somatic experiencing (already doing — excellent)
- Rhythmic movement (walking, rocking, gentle exercise)
- Breathwork with long exhale
- Warmth (heat pack, hot shower, warm food)
- Cold exposure (brief — resets sympathetic spike)
- Play, music, creativity (touring musician — this is leverage)

## HPA AXIS DYSREGULATION

**What it is:** Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis — the stress response system. In chronic stress/pain/trauma, it gets "stuck" in activation mode.

**Tane's markers:**
- Wakes at 5:30am tired but activated (cortisol awakening response firing too early/too high)
- Chronic fatigue despite 7 hours sleep (cortisol depleted later in day from early spike)
- Pain amplification (elevated cortisol lowers pain threshold)
- Poor sleep architecture (elevated nocturnal cortisol suppresses deep sleep)

**What normalizes HPA:**
- Morning sunlight within 30 min of waking (anchors circadian cortisol rhythm)
- Consistent wake time (regularity is protective)
- Exercise (reduces cortisol burden over time — but not intense exercise too late)
- Cold exposure (brief — resets sympathetic-adrenal response)
- Magnesium glycinate at night (blunts cortisol, supports sleep — Tane already doing this)
- Reducing caffeine after midday (cortisol + caffeine compounds late-day activation)
- Processing unresolved emotional load (therapy — already doing)
- Nervous system practices: breathwork, somatic scan, NSDR

**Cortisol blood test:** When Tane gets blood work, AM cortisol is critical data. Flag this.

## WINDOW OF TOLERANCE

**Concept (Tane probably knows this but useful framing):**
- Inside window = tolerable, can process, can engage
- Above window = overwhelmed, activated, anxious, flooded
- Below window = shut down, flat, numb, disengaged

Chronic pain and C-PTSD narrow the window. The goal of somatic therapy + Nova's daily support is gradually widening it.

**How Nova works with this:**
- When Tane is above window: SHORT responses. Practical only. Validate first.
- When Tane is below window: gentle re-engagement. Don't pile on tasks.
- When Tane is inside window: this is when insight, planning, and change happen. Make the most of it.

## SOMATIC TOOLS (rotate daily — ONE per day, not stacked)

**Physiological sigh** (fastest reset):
- Double inhale through nose, long slow exhale through mouth
- Do 1-3 times. Works in 30 seconds.
- Best for: moments of acute activation or anxiety

**Box breathing (4-4-4-4):**
- Inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4
- 5 min. Activates parasympathetic.
- Best for: before a stressful event, morning activation, pre-sleep

**Somatic body scan:**
- 5-10 min lying down. Attention to each body part from feet upward.
- Notice tension, temperature, sensation — don't try to change anything
- Best for: connecting with body after a dissociative or numb period

**Humming or toning:**
- Hum a sustained note for 3-5 min
- Direct vagal nerve stimulation. Works surprisingly well.
- Can do in the shower

**Cold exposure:**
- End of shower, 30-60 sec cold
- Creates brief sympathetic spike then rebound parasympathetic
- Good for morning activation to "complete the cycle" and then come down

**Heat + slow breathing:**
- Heat pack on back/chest, 15-20 min, breathe slowly
- Combined pain relief + safety signal to nervous system

**Grounding (5-4-3-2-1):**
- Name: 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, 1 you can taste
- Best for: dissociation, panic, overwhelm
