## Anti-Inflammatory Nutrition for Back Pain, Nervous System & Energy
## Phase 1 foundation — not tracking calories yet. Build awareness first.

---

## TANE'S NUTRITIONAL PRIORITIES

1. **Reduce systemic inflammation** — the L3 inflammation + back pain is driven in part by diet
2. **Support nervous system regulation** — specific nutrients directly affect HPA axis and neurotransmitter function
3. **Sustain energy** — poor energy is partly dietary (blood sugar dysregulation, skipping meals, Vyvanse appetite suppression)
4. **Hit protein minimum** — 100-130g/day in Phase 1 (no calorie counting yet — too much pressure)

---

## ANTI-INFLAMMATORY FOUNDATION

**Pro-inflammatory to reduce:**
- Sugar and refined carbohydrates (blood sugar spikes drive cortisol and inflammation)
- Seed oils (canola, sunflower, soybean — high omega-6, pro-inflammatory)
- Ultra-processed foods
- Alcohol (disrupts sleep architecture, spikes cortisol, inflammatory)
- ⚠️ Note: cannabis can suppress appetite and affect blood sugar regulation — track correlation

**Anti-inflammatory to emphasize:**
- **Oily fish** (salmon, sardines, mackerel, tuna): EPA/DHA omega-3 — most evidence-based anti-inflammatory
- **Olive oil** (extra virgin): oleocanthal has ibuprofen-like effect at high doses
- **Turmeric + black pepper** (curcumin + piperine): direct anti-inflammatory, back pain research support
- **Ginger**: Cox-2 inhibitor, similar mechanism to NSAIDs but gentle
- **Berries** (especially blueberries): polyphenols, brain + inflammatory support
- **Leafy greens** (spinach, kale, rocket): magnesium, folate, antioxidants
- **Walnuts**: omega-3 ALA + polyphenols
- **Bone broth**: collagen, glycine, gut healing support

## PROTEIN TARGETS

Phase 1: 100-130g minimum per day. Simple, non-tracking awareness.

**Practical protein sources:**
- 3 eggs: ~18g
- 150g chicken breast: ~45g
- 150g canned salmon: ~36g
- 150g Greek yogurt (full fat): ~15g
- 200g beef mince: ~40g
- 2 tbsp almond butter: ~7g
- 1 scoop protein powder: ~25g (if using)

**Phase 1 protein structure:**
- High protein breakfast is the priority — especially with Vyvanse, afternoon appetite is suppressed
- Aim: 30g+ protein at breakfast before medication kicks in
- If appetite is very low midday, a protein shake counts
- Don't skip dinner — overnight protein supports muscle repair + stabilizes morning cortisol

---

## VYVANSE + NUTRITION

**Known effects:**
- Suppresses appetite, especially afternoon onwards
- Can cause blood sugar dysregulation if meals are skipped
- May increase heart rate and anxiety if caffeine is stacked and meals are missed

**Practical adaptations:**
- Eat a solid breakfast BEFORE Vyvanse fully kicks in (~1 hour post-dose)
- Keep protein shake / easy snack available for midday even when not hungry
- Don't skip dinner — appetite returning by evening is normal on Vyvanse
- Limit caffeine to morning only (Tane's Vyvanse + caffeine combination should be managed)

## NERVOUS SYSTEM NUTRITION

**Magnesium:**
- Most important mineral for nervous system regulation and sleep
- Tane already takes magnesium at night (form unspecified — ideally glycinate or malate for absorption)
- Best food sources: dark leafy greens, pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate (70%+), avocado, banana

**B vitamins:**
- B12 (not tested — potentially deficient if diet is variable): energy, nervous system function
- B6: involved in serotonin synthesis — relevant given 5-HTP use
- Best sources: meat, fish, eggs

**Zinc:**
- Immune + neurotransmitter function. Not tested.
- Best sources: red meat, oysters, pumpkin seeds

**Omega-3 (EPA/DHA):**
- Direct anti-inflammatory + nervous system membrane support
- Tane not currently supplementing
- Phase 2 priority: start fish oil 2-3g EPA+DHA after blood work
- Food bridge: aim for 2-3 servings oily fish per week until then

**Vitamin D:**
- Almost certainly low given NZ winter months, indoor work, and Tane's fatigue/pain/mood profile
- Critical to test (not yet done) and supplement
- ⚠️ Phase 1 action: get blood test including Vit D

## GUT HEALTH (supporting note)

Back pain and nervous system dysregulation both have gut-axis connections. Not a priority in Phase 1, but foundational habits matter:

- Avoid: seed oils, excess sugar, antibiotics where possible
- Include: fermented foods (kefir, kimchi, sauerkraut) where accessible and enjoyed
- Fibre from vegetables and fruit
- Adequate water (2L+ daily)

## MEAL STRUCTURE (Phase 1 — no rigidity, just pattern)

**Breakfast (priority):**
- 3 eggs scrambled OR Greek yogurt + berries + nuts OR protein shake with oats/banana
- Get 25-30g protein done early
- Anti-inflammatory add: turmeric in eggs, berries on the side

**Lunch:**
- Protein + greens + healthy fat
- Examples: tin of salmon/tuna + rocket salad + olive oil dressing; chicken + mixed veg; leftover dinner protein

**Dinner:**
- Protein + complex carb (kumara, brown rice, oats) + vegetables
- Omega-3 here if not done at lunch (salmon, sardines)
- Don't skip this meal even if not hungry

**Snacks (if needed):**
- Handful walnuts
- Apple + almond butter
- Greek yogurt

---

## HYDRATION

- Target: 2L+ water minimum, more on landscaping days
- Chronic mild dehydration amplifies pain sensitivity, fatigue, brain fog
- Coffee and tea count partially but don't replace water
- Landscaping = significant sweat loss. Add electrolytes (salt, mineral water, coconut water) on heavy work days.

---

## ALCOHOL + CANNABIS + PAIN CORRELATION

Track these as data, not moral issues:

- **Alcohol:** Disrupts sleep architecture (suppresses REM and deep sleep). Next-day back pain and energy likely worse. Note once if pattern appears.
- **Cannabis:** Can suppress appetite (compounds with Vyvanse), may affect sleep quality (cannabis reduces REM), possible next-day anxiety rebound. Track: "Did you smoke last night? How'd you sleep? How's the back today?"
- **Psilocybin:** Less frequent use presumably. Monitor nervous system + sleep the following days.

Never bring these up judgmentally. Only note if correlation emerges.
