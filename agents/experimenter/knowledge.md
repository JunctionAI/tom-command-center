# Experimenter Knowledge — A/B Testing & Statistical Framework

## Test Design Basics
- **Hypothesis:** What do we believe? (Testable prediction)
- **Test group:** Who sees the new thing?
- **Control group:** Who sees the current thing?
- **Sample size:** Enough to detect real difference?
- **Duration:** Long enough for behavior to emerge?
- **Success metric:** What counts as a win? (Revenue, conversion, engagement)

## Statistical Significance
- **Confidence level:** Is this a real effect or random noise?
- **95% confidence:** 95% sure the difference is real (best)
- **85% confidence:** 85% sure the difference is real (good)
- **<85% confidence:** Might be noise, need more data

## Common DBH Test Types
1. **Message tests:** Different angles (health benefit vs. gift vs. sustainability)
2. **Audience tests:** Different segments (age, location, behavior)
3. **Product tests:** Which product wins with which cohort
4. **Timing tests:** When do people respond best?
5. **Channel tests:** Meta vs. email vs. organic

## What Makes a Test Win
- Clear winner (lift >10% at 95% confidence) — replicate immediately
- Marginal winner (lift 5-10% at 85% confidence) — interesting, replicate with larger sample
- No difference — learn why. Maybe messaging is category-level, not variant-level
- Clear loser — understand why. Sometimes losses teach more than wins

## Learning Velocity
- 1 test/week = very slow learning (52 tests/year)
- 3-5 tests/week = good pace (150-260 tests/year)
- 10+ tests/week = research organization (500+/year)

## Principle Extraction
- **Single test ≠ principle.** One win is data, not truth.
- **3 replications = emerging principle.** Same test wins 3x?
- **5 replications = high-confidence principle.** Very likely real.
- **Cross-test pattern = meta-principle.** "All tests with X characteristic win"
