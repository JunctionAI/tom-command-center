## PROMPT 1: FRAME-BY-FRAME CREATIVE BREAKDOWN

```
I'm uploading a 14-second vertical video ad for a pet joint supplement brand called "Pure Pets" (by Deep Blue Health, New Zealand). This ran as a Meta (Facebook/Instagram) ad.

Please provide a detailed frame-by-frame breakdown:

1. SCENE LOG: Describe every distinct scene/shot with timestamps (e.g., 0:00-0:03). Note:
   - What's visually on screen (animals, people, products, environments)
   - Camera angle and movement
   - Any text overlays, graphics, or logos
   - Transitions between scenes
   - Audio: voiceover, music, sound effects

2. HOOK ANALYSIS (first 3 seconds):
   - What is the visual hook? What's the first thing the viewer sees?
   - Is there an emotional trigger in the first frame?
   - Would this stop a pet owner from scrolling? Why or why not?
   - Rate the hook strength 1-10 and explain your rating

3. PACING ASSESSMENT:
   - Is the video too fast, too slow, or right for a social media ad?
   - Are there any dead moments where attention could drop?
   - Does the visual rhythm match the emotional arc?

4. CTA MOMENT:
   - When does the call-to-action appear?
   - Is it clear what the viewer should do next?
   - Is the CTA benefit-focused ("See Results") or transactional ("Shop Now")?

5. PRODUCTION QUALITY:
   - Rate overall production value 1-10
   - Does it feel authentic/UGC-style or corporate/polished?
   - For Meta ads, authentic/UGC-style typically outperforms polished — which side does this fall on?
```

## PROMPT 3: AUDIENCE PSYCHOLOGY ANALYSIS

```
Analyse this pet supplement video ad through behavioural psychology and direct response advertising frameworks:

1. EUGENE SCHWARTZ AWARENESS LEVELS:
   The 5 levels of customer awareness are:
   - Most Aware (knows the brand, just needs a deal)
   - Product Aware (knows the product exists, needs convincing)
   - Solution Aware (knows solutions exist, comparing options)
   - Problem Aware (knows their dog has joint issues, doesn't know the solution)
   - Unaware (doesn't realise their dog's issues are treatable)

   Which awareness level does this video target? Is that the right choice?
   For context: most pet parents with older dogs are Level 4 (Problem Aware) — they SEE the problem daily.

2. KAHNEMAN COGNITIVE BIASES:
   Which of these biases does the video activate (or fail to activate)?
   - Loss aversion: "Don't let your dog lose mobility" (2x stronger than gain framing)
   - Social proof: showing other customers' results
   - Identifiable victim: ONE specific dog's story vs statistics
   - Anchoring: price/value framing
   - Guilt as motivator: "They can't choose for themselves"
   Rate each: Strongly Used / Weakly Used / Not Used / Could Be Used

3. SABRI SUBY HBSP FORMULA:
   Rate each element 1-10:
   - H (Hook): Does it stop the scroll in <2 seconds?
   - B (Believability): Does it explain WHY the product works?
   - S (Social Proof): Are other customers' results visible?
   - P (Push): Is there a compelling CTA with risk-reversal?

4. SHAREABILITY (Emily Weiss / Glossier model):
   - Would a real pet parent share this video organically?
   - Does it feel like content or like an ad?
   - What would make it more shareable?

5. THE EMOTIONAL ARC:
   Map the emotional journey of the video:
   - Does it start with PAIN (the problem)?
   - Does it show HOPE (the transformation)?
   - Does it deliver PROOF (real results)?
   - Does it end with ACTION (clear next step)?
   What's missing from this arc?
```

For each of the 5 concepts, provide:

a) CONCEPT NAME: (e.g., "The Transformation Story", "The Guilt Opener")

b) OPENING HOOK (0-3 seconds):
   - Exact visual description of frame 1
   - What emotion does it trigger?
   - Why would a pet owner stop scrolling?

c) TRANSFORMATION ARC (3-15 seconds):
   - What story does the middle section tell?
   - How does it demonstrate the product working?
   - What visual evidence of change is shown?

d) PROOF ELEMENT (15-22 seconds):
   - How is social proof / trust established?
   - Is there a testimonial, statistic, or guarantee?
   - What makes the viewer believe this works?

e) CTA (22-30 seconds):
   - What specific action is the viewer prompted to take?
   - Is there urgency or risk-reversal?

f) EXPECTED IMPROVEMENT:
   - Rate this concept vs the original (% improvement estimate)
   - Which specific weakness of the original does it fix?

g) PRODUCTION NOTES:
   - Could this be produced with AI video tools (Higgsfield, Kling)?
   - What elements would need to be real (customer footage, product shots)?
   - Difficulty level: Easy / Medium / Hard

Rank all 5 concepts by expected performance, best first.
