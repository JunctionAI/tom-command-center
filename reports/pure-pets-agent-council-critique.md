## LENS 1: EUGENE SCHWARTZ (Awareness Levels)
**Score: 6/10**

**What awareness level does this target?**
The opening hook ("Is your dog slowing down?") targets Level 4: Problem Aware. This is correct — most pet parents with older dogs see the problem daily.

**What's right:**
- The question hook speaks directly to the symptom pet parents observe
- The visual of a dog struggling at stairs is the exact pain point
- This is the right awareness level for cold audience (TOF)

**What's wrong:**
- The video then jumps to Level 5 (Unaware) territory — the NZ landscape/ingredient footage is educational content about WHERE the ingredient comes from. Problem-Aware viewers don't care about ingredient sourcing yet. They care about "will it fix my dog?"
- Schwartz would say: "You started with the pain, then abandoned it for a lecture on marine biology."
- The transformation (beach scene) comes too late and has no proof attached to it

**Schwartz's fix:**
Stay in Level 4 the entire time. Hook with the problem, show the transformation, prove it with a real result. Save the ingredient story for retargeting (Level 3: Solution Aware viewers who already want to buy and need to know WHY this works).

The opening hook feels close to authentic (dog at stairs = relatable). But the NZ landscape sequence breaks it completely. Then the "owner" appears but she looks like a model/AI character, not a real customer.

**Critical issue: the dog changes.**
The Labrador at the stairs is different from the Golden Retriever on the beach. The colouring shifts, the build changes. For a before/after narrative, this breaks the illusion. Pet parents will notice.

## THE THREE FATAL FLAWS

**1. ZERO SOCIAL PROOF**
Every framework flags this. It's the single biggest difference between your 7.78x ROAS campaign and your 1.4x ROAS campaign. No customer name, no testimonial, no review, no "trusted by X dogs." Fix this one thing and performance likely doubles.

**2. THE EDUCATION SECTION BREAKS THE EMOTIONAL ARC**
The video hooks with emotion (sad dog at stairs), then abandons it for 3 seconds of NZ scenery and underwater mussels. This is where most viewers leave. The emotional thread (struggling → healed) gets interrupted by a nature documentary. Cut this section entirely for TOF.

**3. NO CTA / NO BRAND / NO NEXT STEP**
The video ends with a happy dog on a beach. Beautiful. But the viewer doesn't know: what product is this? Where do I buy it? What should I do? There's no text overlay, no brand name, no "See Results" button, no website. The viewer enjoyed the video and scrolled on.

## WHAT THE DATA WOULD CONFIRM

When you pull the Meta video metrics, check:
1. **Hook rate (3-sec views / impressions):** If >25%, the opening is working. If <20%, even the hook needs improvement.
2. **Drop-off at 25% mark (~3.5s):** If massive drop here, it's the landscape transition killing it.
3. **Drop-off at 50% mark (~7s):** If viewers survive to 50%, the mussels section didn't kill them but the damage was done earlier.
4. **Completion rate:** If <10%, the video structure is fundamentally wrong. If >20%, the hook is carrying it despite the flaws.
5. **ThruPlay vs purchases correlation:** If people who complete the video still don't buy, the issue is the missing CTA/social proof at the end.
