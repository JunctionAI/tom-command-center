## WHAT WORKED IN THE ORIGINAL VIDEO

1. **The stairs hook is effective.** A sad dog looking up at stairs it can't climb is an immediate emotional trigger. Every pet parent with an older dog recognises this scene. This should be KEPT in variations.

2. **The beach payoff is aspirational.** Dog running free on a NZ black sand beach at golden hour = the outcome every pet parent wants. This ending works as an emotional reward.

3. **The capsule-feeding scene is natural.** Hand offering a supplement to a dog is relatable and non-salesy. Shows the product without being product-focused.

4. **Overall AI video quality is strong.** The generation quality is high enough that it doesn't read as obviously fake (except the breed change — see below).

---

## WHAT DIDN'T WORK (Root Cause Analysis)

**Flaw 1: Zero social proof**
- Root cause: The video tells a generic story, not a REAL story. No customer name, no testimonial, no "trusted by" claim.
- Evidence: GLM Social Proof campaign (7.78x ROAS) vs Pure Pets Product Focus (1.4x ROAS). The ONLY major difference is social proof.
- Fix: Add customer identity (name, quote, real result) either as voiceover or text overlay.

**Flaw 2: Education section interrupts emotional arc**
- Root cause: 3 seconds of NZ landscape + underwater mussels is ingredient education. Problem-Aware viewers (Level 4) don't need to know WHERE the ingredient comes from — they need to know that it WORKS.
- Evidence: This is where the video drops from emotional to educational. The hook promises "your dog is slowing down" — the viewer expects to see what to do about it, not a marine biology lesson.
- Fix: Remove the landscape/mussels section entirely from TOF. Replace with transformation proof or testimonial.

**Flaw 3: Dog changes breed**
- Root cause: AI generation created a Labrador for the stairs scene and a Golden Retriever for the beach scene. Different colouring, build, and age appearance.
- Evidence: Pet parents are extremely observant about dog breeds. This breaks the before/after narrative.
- Fix: Use consistent character (same breed, same colouring) throughout. Lock the character in Higgsfield.

**Flaw 4: No CTA, no brand, no next step**
- Root cause: Video ends on a beautiful shot but with zero direction. No brand name, no website, no "See Results" button.
- Evidence: Every framework (Suby, playbook, Schwartz) requires a clear next step.
- Fix: Add end card with brand + CTA text overlay. "Pure Pets — See Results" or "purepets.nz — Try Risk-Free."

**Flaw 5: No voiceover**
- Root cause: The video relies entirely on a single text question + visuals. No narration to guide the emotional arc or add proof points.
- Evidence: Video ads with voiceover consistently outperform silent/text-only on Meta (higher engagement, better completion).
- Fix: Add short voiceover that follows the emotional arc.

## NEW HYPOTHESES

### HYPOTHESIS A: Social Proof Overlay Doubles ROAS
**We believe** adding customer testimonial text overlays and a voiceover testimonial to the same video structure **will increase ROAS from 1.4x to 3-4x** because the Trust + Social Proof formula produced 7.78x ROAS on GLM and the ONLY missing element in this video is social proof.

- Control: Original video (no social proof)
- Variant: Same scenes + text overlays ("Sarah's Lab was struggling to walk. 3 weeks later...") + voiceover
- Win criteria: ROAS > 2x, CPA < $15
- Test budget: $15/day for 5 days

### HYPOTHESIS B: Removing Education Section Improves Completion 2x
**We believe** cutting the NZ landscape/mussels section (3s) and replacing it with the transformation arc **will double video completion rate** because the education section breaks the emotional thread and causes the biggest viewer drop-off.

- Control: Original structure (Hook → Education → Product → Payoff)
- Variant: New structure (Hook → Product → Transformation → CTA)
- Win criteria: Completion rate doubles, ThruPlay rate improves 50%+
- Secondary: Does higher completion = higher conversion?

### HYPOTHESIS C: Consistent Character Improves Trust
**We believe** using the SAME dog breed throughout (no swap from Lab to Golden) **will improve trust metrics (CTR, CPA)** because continuity signals authenticity and pet parents are breed-observant.

- Control: Current video (breed changes)
- Variant: Single breed throughout (Golden Retriever locked in Higgsfield)
- Win criteria: CTR improvement of 20%+

### HYPOTHESIS D: Voiceover + Text Combo Beats Text-Only
**We believe** adding a warm female voiceover narrating a customer story **will improve hook rate and completion** because audio engagement retains attention alongside visual storytelling, and a personal narrative activates Kahneman's identifiable victim effect.

- Control: Text overlay only ("Is your dog slowing down?")
- Variant: Voiceover: "Bella used to love running up these stairs. Then one morning, she just... stopped."
- Win criteria: Hook rate > 30%, completion rate > 25%

### HYPOTHESIS E: Emotional CTA Beats No CTA
**We believe** adding a branded end card with benefit-focused CTA **will improve click-through rate 2-3x** because the current video has no CTA — viewers literally don't know what to do or where to go.

- Control: Video ends on beach scene (no CTA)
- Variant: Final 2 seconds = "Pure Pets" logo + "See what's possible" + website
- Win criteria: CTR > 1.5% (vs likely <0.5% currently)
