# PURE PETS — HIGGSFIELD VIDEO PRODUCTION BRIEFS
## 5 New Video Concepts Ready for Production

**Tool:** Higgsfield (primary) — character creation, scene generation, video assembly
**Format:** 9:16 vertical, 15-30 seconds, 720x1280 minimum
**Audio:** Voiceover to be recorded separately (or AI voice) + background music

---

## CONCEPT 1: "BELLA'S STAIRS" (Social Proof + Transformation)
**Tests:** Hypotheses A + B + D + E combined
**Expected impact:** Highest — addresses all 5 fatal flaws from original

### Character
- **Dog:** Golden Labrador (female), 9-10 years old appearance
  - Warm golden/honey coat, slightly greying around muzzle
  - Brown eyes, expressive face
  - Name: "Bella" (displayed in text)
  - MUST be the same dog in ALL scenes (consistent coat, build, markings)
- **Owner:** Woman, 45-55, warm appearance
  - Brown hair, casual clothing (olive/earth tones)
  - Relatable "real mum" feel, NOT model-perfect
  - Appears in scenes 1 and 4 only

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

**Scene 1 — THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)**
- Start frame: Top of wooden staircase, looking down. Bella sitting at the bottom, looking up with sad/hesitant expression. Morning light through a window.
- Camera: Static, top-down angle (owner's POV looking at dog)
- Text overlay: "Bella used to run up these stairs."
- Voiceover: "Bella used to run up these stairs. Then one morning... she just stopped."
- Mood: Quiet, bittersweet. Piano note or soft strings.
- Motion: Minimal. Dog shifts weight, looks away then back up. Doesn't attempt stairs.

**Scene 2 — THE SOLUTION (3-6 seconds)**
- Start frame: Close-up of owner's hand holding an orange gel capsule. Bella's nose entering frame.
- Camera: Macro/close-up, shallow depth of field
- Text overlay: "NZ Green Lipped Mussel — natural joint support"
- Voiceover: "Pure Pets uses New Zealand Green Lipped Mussel."
- Motion: Dog takes capsule from hand, gentle lick.

**Scene 3 — THE TRANSFORMATION (6-12 seconds)**
- Start frame: Same staircase from scene 1, but now bright morning light floods in.
- Camera: Same top-down angle as scene 1 (deliberate visual mirror)
- Motion sequence: Bella BOUNDS up the stairs, tail wagging, reaches the top
- Text overlay: "3 weeks later" (appears at start of scene, fades)
- Voiceover: "Three weeks later, Bella was back."
- Quick cut to: Bella running on a NZ black sand beach, golden hour, tongue out, joyful
- Text overlay: "Real results. Real dogs."
- Voiceover: "Real results from real New Zealand ingredients."

**Scene 4 — THE PROOF + CTA (12-15 seconds)**
- Start frame: Owner kneeling, Bella running to her on the beach
- Camera: Wide shot, then zoom to medium as dog reaches owner
- Text overlay: "Trusted by 5,000+ NZ pet parents"
- Voiceover: "Join thousands of Kiwi pet parents who chose Pure Pets."
- Final frame (hold 2s): Pure Pets logo + "See Results at purepets.co.nz" + "Made in New Zealand"
- Music: Swells to warm resolution

### Production Notes
- The stairs scene MUST use the same camera angle in scenes 1 and 3 — the visual mirror is the emotional payoff
- The dog MUST be identical in all scenes — same coat colour, same greying muzzle, same build
- Beach scene should be recognisably NZ (black sand, native bush headland)
- Text overlays: white, clean sans-serif font, subtle drop shadow. NOT flashy or animated.
- Total duration target: 15 seconds (tight, no wasted frames)

## CONCEPT 2: "THE VET VISIT" (Loss Aversion + Identifiable Victim)
**Tests:** Kahneman's loss aversion + identifiable victim effect
**Expected impact:** High — activates the strongest psychological lever

### Character
- **Dog:** Border Collie, 8 years old, black and white
  - Intelligent face, distinctive markings
  - Name: "Max" (displayed in text)
- **Owner:** Man, 40s, outdoor/farmer type
  - Wearing work shirt, tanned. Kiwi bloke archetype.

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

**Scene 1 — THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)**
- Start frame: Close-up of Max lying on a couch, chin on paws, eyes looking up. Not moving.
- Text overlay: "Max stopped chasing the ball."
- Voiceover (male, NZ accent): "One day Max just... stopped wanting to play."
- Mood: Quiet concern. No music, just ambient room sound.

**Scene 2 — THE FEAR (3-5 seconds)**
- Start frame: Max at the vet. Vet examining his leg/joints. Owner watching, worried expression.
- Text overlay: "The vet said joint deterioration."
- Voiceover: "The vet said it was his joints. And it would only get worse."
- Camera: Over-the-shoulder of owner watching vet.

**Scene 3 — THE DISCOVERY (5-8 seconds)**
- Start frame: Owner at home, researching on laptop. Pure Pets product visible on table.
- Camera: Medium shot, warm home lighting
- Text overlay: "Then we found Pure Pets."
- Voiceover: "A mate recommended Pure Pets. NZ Green Lipped Mussel, pharmaceutical-grade."
- Motion: Owner picks up product, reads label, nods.

**Scene 4 — THE TRANSFORMATION (8-12 seconds)**
- Start frame: Park/farm setting. Max with a ball in his mouth, running toward camera.
- Camera: Low angle (dog's eye level), tracking shot
- Text overlay: "4 weeks later"
- Voiceover: "Four weeks. That's all it took."
- Motion: Max sprinting, jumping, catching ball mid-air. Full energy.
- Quick cut: Max and owner playing fetch on a farm paddock, green NZ hills behind.

**Scene 5 — CTA (12-15 seconds)**
- Owner crouching, Max jumping on him, licking face
- Text overlay: "Don't wait until it gets worse."
- Voiceover: "Don't wait. See results at purepets.co.nz."
- Final frame: Logo + "See Results" + "Made in NZ"

### Production Notes
- Loss aversion is the key lever: "It will only get WORSE" is 2x more motivating than "it could get better"
- The vet scene adds believability (Suby's B in HBSP)
- Male owner + Border Collie = different audience segment than Concept 1 (female + Lab)
- NZ farm setting = differentiator from generic pet supplement ads

## CONCEPT 3: "MORNING ROUTINE" (UGC-Style, Community-Feel)
**Tests:** Emily Weiss authenticity model + shareability
**Expected impact:** Medium-High — designed for organic sharing alongside paid

### Character
- **Dog:** Small mixed breed / Jack Russell type, older (greying, 10+ years)
  - Name: "Pippa"
  - Character: Scrappy, determined little dog — underdog energy
- **Owner:** Woman, 60s, grandmother type
  - Silver hair, warm cardigan, reading glasses on chain
  - Genuine, not polished. "Your actual nan" energy.

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

**Scene 1 — THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)**
- Start frame: Kitchen bench, morning light. A hand (owner's) placing a Pure Pets capsule next to a coffee cup. Pippa's face peeking over the bench edge.
- Camera: Phone-quality feel (slightly shaky, natural light, not perfect framing)
- Text overlay: "Our morning routine. Every single day."
- Voiceover (older NZ woman): "Pippa gets hers before I get mine."
- Mood: Warm, homey, real. Background sounds of a kettle.

**Scene 2 — THE STORY (3-7 seconds)**
- Pippa eating the capsule, then looking up expectantly
- Owner's hand patting Pippa's head
- Text overlay: "She nearly couldn't walk 6 months ago."
- Voiceover: "Six months ago, she could barely make it to the letterbox."
- Camera: Close-up of Pippa's face, then pull back to show the kitchen scene

**Scene 3 — THE PAYOFF (7-12 seconds)**
- Garden setting. Pippa trotting along a path, sniffing flowers, tail wagging
- Owner walking slowly behind with a cup of tea
- Text overlay: "Now she runs to the letterbox and back."
- Voiceover: "Now she does the whole loop. Twice if I let her."
- Camera: Following Pippa, low angle, garden path

**Scene 4 — CTA (12-15 seconds)**
- Owner sitting in garden chair, Pippa on her lap, both looking at camera
- Text overlay: "Pure Pets. Made in NZ. For your best friend."
- Voiceover: "Made right here in New Zealand. For your best friend."
- Hold on this frame for 2 seconds with website URL

### Production Notes
- This concept is designed to feel like a REAL customer video, not an ad
- Phone-quality camera feel is intentional — authenticity > production value
- Smaller/older dog = different audience (not just Golden Retriever owners)
- Grandmother archetype = high trust, high shareability
- The "morning routine" framing makes it feel like content, not an ad (Weiss model)
- This is the most shareable concept — pet parents will tag friends

## CONCEPT 4: "SPLIT SCREEN" (Before/After Data-Driven)
**Tests:** Hypothesis B (remove education, pure transformation) + visual proof
**Expected impact:** Medium — strong for retargeting audience

### Character
- **Dog:** German Shepherd, 7 years old
  - Name: "Rex"
  - Strong, noble breed — shows contrast when struggling vs thriving

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

**Scene 1 — SPLIT SCREEN HOOK (0-5 seconds)**
- Start frame: Screen split vertically 50/50
  - LEFT side (muted colours, slightly dark): Rex lying on a bed, not wanting to get up
  - RIGHT side (warm colours, bright): Rex running through a park, powerful stride
- Text overlay top: "BEFORE PURE PETS / AFTER PURE PETS"
- Text overlay bottom: "Rex, 7 years old, Auckland"
- Voiceover: "Same dog. Same age. The only difference? Pure Pets."
- Camera: LEFT = static, dog not moving. RIGHT = tracking shot, dog in motion.

**Scene 2 — THE PRODUCT (5-8 seconds)**
- Full frame: Rex eating the capsule from owner's hand in a kitchen
- Text overlay: "NZ Green Lipped Mussel — supports joint mobility naturally"
- Voiceover: "New Zealand's Green Lipped Mussel. Pharmaceutical-grade."

**Scene 3 — THE PROOF (8-12 seconds)**
- Full frame: Rex at a dog park, playing with another dog, jumping for a stick
- Text overlay cycling through:
  - "5,000+ NZ dogs"
  - "4.8 star average"
  - "Results in 3-4 weeks"
- Voiceover: "Join five thousand Kiwi dogs already feeling the difference."

**Scene 4 — CTA (12-15 seconds)**
- Rex close-up, happy face, tongue out
- Pure Pets logo + "See Results" + website
- Voiceover: "See results. purepets.co.nz"

### Production Notes
- Split screen is a proven high-engagement format on Meta (visual pattern interrupt)
- The simultaneous before/after is immediately understood — no narrative needed
- This concept works well as a 6-second cutdown (just the split screen + logo)
- German Shepherd = third breed across concepts (Lab, Collie, Shepherd, Jack Russell = broad appeal)

## CONCEPT 5: "THE GUILT HOOK" (Kahneman Loss Aversion, Maximum Emotional Impact)
**Tests:** Guilt as motivator + loss aversion + identifiable victim
**Expected impact:** High for engagement, potentially polarising (test carefully)

### Character
- **Dog:** Golden Retriever, 10 years old
  - Name: "Charlie"
  - Sweet, gentle face. The "old good boy" archetype.
- **Child:** Girl, 8-10 years old
  - Charlie's human. They grew up together.

### Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

**Scene 1 — THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)**
- Start frame: A young girl kneeling, holding a ball, looking at Charlie who is lying on the grass, not getting up. Backyard setting.
- Text overlay: "He can't play with her anymore."
- Voiceover (female, quiet, emotional): "Charlie and Sophie grew up together."
- Camera: Wide shot, shallow depth of field. Girl in focus, dog slightly soft.
- Mood: Silence except voiceover. No music. The quiet IS the emotion.

**Scene 2 — THE WEIGHT (3-6 seconds)**
- Girl's hand gently stroking Charlie's head. Charlie's eyes looking up.
- Text overlay: "They can't choose for themselves. You can."
- Voiceover: "He couldn't tell us his joints were hurting. We had to notice."
- Camera: Close-up of hand on dog's head, then dog's eyes.

**Scene 3 — THE TURN (6-10 seconds)**
- Owner (parent) offering Pure Pets capsule to Charlie
- Then: Charlie slowly standing up, taking a few steps
- Then: Charlie walking toward Sophie, who is waiting with the ball
- Text overlay: "Pure Pets. 3 weeks."
- Voiceover: "Three weeks on Pure Pets."
- Music: First note of warm piano enters here

**Scene 4 — THE RELEASE (10-13 seconds)**
- Charlie and Sophie playing in the backyard. Charlie catching the ball. Girl laughing.
- Camera: Tracking shot, golden hour
- Text overlay: "Back to best friends."
- Voiceover: "He's her Charlie again."
- Music: Full warm resolution

**Scene 5 — CTA (13-15 seconds)**
- Charlie and Sophie curled up together on a couch
- Text overlay: "Pure Pets — Made in NZ for your best friend"
- Voiceover: "Made in New Zealand. For your best friend."
- Website URL + "See Results"

### Production Notes
- This is the highest-emotion concept. The child-dog bond is the most powerful trigger in pet marketing.
- "They can't choose for themselves. You can." is Kahneman's guilt lever with compassion (not manipulation).
- Test carefully — some viewers may find it too emotional. That's actually good for engagement.
- If it works, this becomes the anchor creative. Refresh with different child-dog pairs.
- The silence-to-music transition at the turn point (scene 3) is a proven emotional technique.

## VOICEOVER NOTES

For all concepts:
- **Accent:** New Zealand (not American, not British)
- **Tone:** Warm, genuine, slightly emotional but not overdone
- **Pace:** Conversational, not rushed. Let pauses breathe.
- **AI voice option:** ElevenLabs or similar — use a warm NZ female voice for concepts 1, 3, 5. NZ male voice for concept 2. No voiceover needed for concept 4 (text-driven).

---

## MUSIC NOTES

- **Concepts 1, 3, 5:** Soft piano or acoustic guitar. Starts quiet, builds to warm resolution.
- **Concept 2:** More dramatic. Starts with silence/ambient, builds tension, releases at transformation.
- **Concept 4:** Upbeat, confident. The split screen format needs energy.
- Source: Artlist, Epidemic Sound, or AI-generated (Suno, Udio)
