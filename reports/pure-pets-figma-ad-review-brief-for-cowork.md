## PART 2: THE WINNING FRAMEWORK YOU'RE TESTING AGAINST

### Three-Part Formula That Works:

**1. HEADLINE:** [Customer Name/Story] + [Specific Problem Solved]
```
Examples of GOOD headlines (Trust formula):
✓ "See what Pure Pets did for Bella's joints"
✓ "Max couldn't jump. Then we tried Pure Pets."
✓ "After 3 weeks, Bella was running again"

Examples of BAD headlines (Product-focus formula — AVOID):
✗ "Pure Pets Bi-Active for Joint Health"
✗ "Natural Joint Support for Dogs"
✗ "Premium Dog Joint Supplement"
```

**2. VISUAL:** Real dog owner or customer dog showing improvement
```
GOOD visuals:
✓ Real customer's dog showing mobility (running, jumping, playing)
✓ Before/after of same dog (struggling → energetic)
✓ Customer with their dog, authentic home setting
✓ Close-up of dog's face showing engagement/happiness

BAD visuals (what NOT to use):
✗ Product-only shot (bottle, jar, powder)
✗ Stock photography (generic beautiful dog)
✗ Model/actor with dog (not authentic)
✗ Professional studio lighting (kills authenticity)
✗ Glossy, overly-produced images
```

**3. PRIMARY TEXT (Body Copy):** Problem → Proof → Benefit → Trust
```
GOOD copy structure:
✓ Starts with PROBLEM: "Is your dog struggling with mobility?"
✓ Adds SOCIAL PROOF: "Here's what real customers are seeing..."
✓ Specific BENEFIT: "Natural joint support that keeps them active"
✓ TRUST SIGNAL: "Made in NZ. Pharmaceutical-grade."
✓ Example:
   "Is your dog struggling with mobility?
    Here's what real customers are seeing...
    After 3 weeks on Pure Pets, their dogs were jumping again.
    Natural joint support made in NZ.
    See Results"

BAD copy structure (product-first, education-only):
✗ Leads with product: "Pure Pets is a premium supplement..."
✗ No problem hook: Doesn't identify pain point
✗ Missing social proof: No customer stories
✗ Generic benefits: "Wellness support" vs "Mobility improvement"
✗ Transactional CTA: "Shop Now" vs "See Results"
```

**4. CTA Button:**
```
GOOD CTA (what converts quality customers):
✓ "See Results" — benefit-focused
✓ "Learn How" — educational angle
✓ "Shop Pure Pets" — if using social proof

BAD CTA (what attracts price shoppers):
✗ "Shop Now" — transactional
✗ "Buy Today" — urgency-only, no benefit
✗ "Discover More" — too vague
```
